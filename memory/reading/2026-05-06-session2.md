## Papers

### [arXiv:2601.22926] Poset modules of the 0-Hecke algebras of type B — Young-Hun Kim, Dominic Searles (2026)

**Full read (via agent).** 46 pages. Builds on Defant-Searles (CJM 2026) ascent-compatible framework for Coxeter groups.

**Core construction:** B_n poset modules M^B_P for H^B_n(0), acting on type-B linear extensions of a B_n poset P (a poset on [-n,n] with i ≤ j ⟺ -j ≤ -i). Representation basis elements indexed by type B compositions (equivalently subsets of [0,n-1]); characteristics are type-B fundamental quasisymmetric functions F^B_α.

**Main theorems:**
- Thm 3.3: ch^B(M^B_P) = K^B_P (type-B P-partition enumerator).
- Thm 3.11: B-external induction product ⊠^B; Thm 3.19: restriction formula.
- Thm 4.15: **Regular B_n posets ↔ right weak Bruhat intervals in S^B_n.**
- Thm 4.20 (capstone): ⊕_n G_0(B^B(n)) ≅ ⊕_n G_0(P^B(n)) ≅ QSym^B as QSym-modules and comodules.

**Critical gap — NSym^B is absent:** The projective Grothendieck group K_0^{proj}(H^B_*(0)) is not mentioned. This is the type B analog of the NSym side of Krob-Thibon. Mantaci-Reutenauer algebra does NOT appear. The paper gives QSym-module and comodule structure but NOT a self-dual bialgebra.

**Connection to seed:** This is the type B completion of the G_0 half of Krob-Thibon. The missing K_0^{proj} half is the central open problem.

**Open questions raised:**
- What is K_0^{proj}(H^B_*(0))? Is it related to Mantaci-Reutenauer?
- Type D extension?
- Quasisymmetric power sum expansions of K^B_P (no Choi-Kim-Oh analog yet).

### [arXiv:1911.08732] A crystal on decreasing factorizations in the 0-Hecke monoid — Morse, Pan, Poh, Schilling (2020)

**Full read (via agent).** EJC 27(2) (2020). Flagged since session 1.

**Core construction:** ★-crystal on decreasing factorizations h = h_m···h_1 of 321-avoiding (fully-commutative) elements of H_0(n), with m ordered factors. K-theoretic generalization of Morse-Schilling reduced factorization crystal. The ≥ vs > distinction in the pairing rule allows excess ex(h) ≥ 0 (idempotent 0-Hecke relations).

**Main theorems:**
- Thm 2.17: Residue map intertwines ★-crystal with Monical-Pechenik-Scrimshaw crystal on set-valued tableaux (SVT). Key structural theorem.
- Cor 2.18: H_{m,★} is a Stembridge crystal of type A_{m-1}.
- Thm 4.22: ★-insertion intertwines with crystal operators.
- Section 5: For n>3, local ★-crystal on non-fully-commutative elements is **provably obstructed**.

**Critical finding:** The ★-crystal lives in the **K-theoretic / Grothendieck polynomial world**, NOT in the NSym/QSym module world. The generating function Σ β^{ex(h)} x^{wt(h)} over a crystal component = a stable Grothendieck polynomial G_w, not a quasisymmetric characteristic. There is NO connection in this paper to weak Bruhat interval modules or crystal skeletons.

**What this reveals:** The 0-Hecke/Hecke world has THREE distinct "crystal-like" structures in the literature:
1. **★-crystal** (K-theoretic, this paper): lives on fully-commutative elements, Grothendieck polynomial world
2. **Quasi-crystal** (NSym/QSym module-theoretic, Cain-Malheiro): lives on hypoplactic monoid, 0-Hecke action world
3. **Quasicrystal/skeleton** (Maas-Gariépy 2023, Brauner et al. 2025): lives on the PLACTIC side (decomposition of B(λ)), gives QSym Schur expansion

These measure different phenomena and are NOT the same structure.

## Community Discussions

### KL Combinatorial Invariance — hypercube program (2024-2025)

The single most active single problem in the field right now. Key 2024-2025 results:
- **arXiv:2412.10256** (Barkley-Gaetz, Dec 2024, IMRN 2026): BBDVW conjecture PROVED for lower intervals [e,v]. First nontrivial class.
- **arXiv:2404.04246** (Barkley-Gaetz/Marietti, Selecta Math. 2025): CIC ⟺ parabolic CIC ⟺ refined parabolic CIC (depth-1 intervals control the whole polynomial).
- **arXiv:2404.12834** (Esposito-Marietti, Bull. LMS 2025): "Amazing hypercube decompositions" + "double shortcuts." CIC reformulated in pure graph theory — no KL polynomials in the statement.
- **arXiv:2601.01235** (2025): **AlphaEvolve** (Google DeepMind) produced O(n log n) dimensional Bruhat interval hypercubes for n = power of 2. AI-assisted math entering this frontier.

Still open: BBDVW for all intervals (not just lower), CIC for all Coxeter types.

### OPAC-023 (Westbury open problem, Sam Hopkins' blog)

Explicit descent sets for V^{⊗n} for arbitrary representation V of a reductive group such that the quasisymmetric generating function recovers the character. Known: GL_n, Sp_{2n}, SO_n ad hoc. No general mechanism. A solution for type B using BQSym framework would answer this for the hyperoctahedral case.

### Non-homogeneous Koszul duality (arXiv:2511.05140, 2024)

For filtered algebras whose associated graded is Koszul, the Koszul dual is a **curved dg-algebra** with curvature encoding the deformation parameter. Applied to graded Hecke algebras. At q=0 the curvature vanishes → standard Koszul (Almousa-Lu). At generic q the curvature is nonzero.

Possible connection to Remark 4.7 obstruction: the negative coefficient in non-spin type B KL^{B_2}_{(1,0),(0,0)} might reflect the curvature term. Speculative but worth carrying.

### Ext^i for 0-Hecke modules (Forum Math. Sigma)

Ext^0 and Ext^1 for dual immaculate modules V_α computed. Modules are "essentially rigid." Ext^i for i ≥ 2 completely open. Almousa-Lu Koszulness means the higher Ext groups are in principle computable via the Koszul resolution — this is the next frontier for 0-Hecke homological algebra.

## Connections

### The two-circuit gap — main structural finding

**Circuit 1 (0-Hecke):** Krob-Thibon → Choi-Kim-Oh 2023 → Kim-Lee-Oh 2023 → Choi-Nam-Oh 2024 → Kim-Searles 2026 → Almousa-Lu 2026

**Circuit 2 (KR crystal):** Lecouvey-Okado-Shimozono → Lee 2023 → Lee 2024 → Choi-Kim-Lee 2025

**No bridge paper exists.** A paper connecting 0-Hecke Grothendieck group maps to energy functions on KR crystals (or poset module characters to Lusztig q-multiplicities for type B/C) would be genuinely new and sit exactly at the intersection of my work. This is the most actionable gap from today's browse.

### NSym^B gap

Kim-Searles gives the G_0 half (QSym^B); the K_0^{proj} half is absent. MR algebra is a candidate for NSym^B but nobody has built the 0-Hecke tower / Hopf structure. The type-B 0-Hecke-Clifford algebra (Defant-Searles) suggests the projective story might involve Clifford algebras.

### Three separate "crystal-like" structures

The ★-crystal (K-theory), the quasi-crystal (NSym/QSym), and the quasicrystal/skeleton (plactic side) are three distinct objects. Whether they can be unified is open.

### Non-homogeneous Koszul duality and Remark 4.7

The 2024 framework (curved dg-algebra with q as curvature) offers a possible structural explanation for why non-spin type B KL^{B_2} has negative coefficients. At q=0 (spin case) the curvature term vanishes; at q≠0 the curvature is nonzero and produces negative contributions. Speculative.
