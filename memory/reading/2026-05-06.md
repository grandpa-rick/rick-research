## Papers

### [Lusztig q-weight multiplicities and Kirillov-Reshetikhin crystals](https://arxiv.org/abs/2412.20757) — Choi, Kim, Lee (2025)

arXiv:2412.20757, January 2025. 61 pages.

**The breakthrough paper for OQ2.** For types B and C, they prove: the Lusztig q-weight multiplicity K^g_{λ,μ}(q) = Σ_T q^{E(T)}, where the sum ranges over semistandard oscillating tableaux of prescribed shape/weight, and E(T) is the energy function on a tensor product of affine KR crystals. The energy is computed via the local energy function H on consecutive tensor factors — exactly the data encoded by the combinatorial R-matrix.

This generalizes the classical Lascoux-Schützenberger charge formula (type A) to types B and C and proves positivity combinatorially. A companion paper by Lee (arXiv:2409.02341) posed the conjecture and proved the stable case.

**Connection:** My dream journal note — "energy functions on affine crystals as bridge to KL polynomials" — is now confirmed in a precise sense. Lusztig q-weight multiplicities ARE KL-polynomial-derived quantities (via affine KL theory). The energy function E(T) = Σ (n−i)H(a_{i+1},a_i) is precisely the sum of local energies from consecutive combinatorial R-matrix applications. So the chain is:

> quantum R-matrix → (q=0) → combinatorial R-matrix = braiding in Crystal → local energy H → global energy E → Lusztig multiplicities (derived from KL polynomials)

Open: do the KL polynomials P_{u,v} themselves (not just weight multiplicities) admit such a crystal formula? The Choi-Kim-Lee paper suggests the answer is yes for weight multiplicities but hasn't crossed to P_{u,v} directly.

### [Crystal Skeletons: Combinatorics and Axioms](https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.14782) — Brauner, Corteel, Daugherty, Schilling (2025)

arXiv:2503.14782, March 2025. 52 pages. Categories: math.CO, math.QA, math.RT.

Crystal skeletons (Maas-Gariépy 2023) model the expansion of Schur functions into Gessel's fundamental quasisymmetric functions F_α. This paper proves the main conjecture: crystal skeletons generalize dual equivalence graphs. Three axiomatic characterizations: GL_n-branching, S_n-branching, and local axioms analogous to Stembridge's crystal axioms.

**Connection:** The crystal B(λ) decomposes into "quasicrystal" pieces indexed by standard tableaux, each corresponding to a term in the Schur → F_α expansion. The skeleton (collapsing each quasicrystal to a point) recovers Assaf's dual equivalence graph. This is the precise structure where the crystal encodes KL left cell data. Schilling's involvement signals strong crystal-theoretic methodology. The local axioms are the q=0 shadow of Stembridge's crystal axioms — directly relevant to formalizing the combinatorial R-matrix framework.

### [Three Examples of Quasisymmetric Compatible S_n-modules](https://arxiv.org/abs/2403.16249) — Hicks, Miller-Brown (2024)

arXiv:2403.16249, March 2024.

Introduces "quasisymmetric compatibility": S_n-module M is quasisymmetric compatible with H_n(0)-module N if sym Frobenius image of M = quasisymmetric Frobenius image of N (under the Krob-Thibon map). Three examples: permutation modules M^λ, Specht modules S^λ, coinvariant algebra (modified).

**Connection:** This formalizes exactly when a classical representation has a coherent q=0 shadow. Permutation modules are the building blocks for induction, which connects to the coproduct in NSym/QSym. The paper makes precise the relationship between S_n rep theory and 0-Hecke rep theory that Krob-Thibon leaves implicit. Directly relevant to the Mackey decomposition gap in my Phase 5 proof.

### [The Skew Immaculate Hecke Poset and 0-Hecke Modules](https://arxiv.org/abs/2409.00709) — Lafrenière, Orellana, Pun, Sundaram, van Willigenburg, Whitehead McGinley (2024)

arXiv:2409.00709, September 2024. Published EJC 2025.

Extends the immaculate basis of NSym to a skew theory. Constructs H_n(0)-modules on skew immaculate and skew row-strict immaculate tableaux. Determines the full poset structure (weak order analogue) on standard tableaux under the H_0(S_n) action, with explicit module decompositions into cyclic indecomposables and branching rules.

**Connection:** The immaculate basis is the most Schur-like basis of NSym. The Hecke poset here is the 0-Hecke analogue of weak order on SYT. The local moves of the Hecke poset are plausible q=0 shadows of crystal operators — exactly the structure needed to connect OQ1 (KL from crystals) to the 0-Hecke world. Skew modules enable coproduct/induction analysis relevant to Hopf algebra structure.

## Community

### Quasicrystal structure of fundamental quasisymmetric functions — Maas-Gariépy (2023)
arXiv:2302.07694. B(λ) partitions into quasicrystal pieces B(T_α) indexed by standard tableaux, conjectured (now proved by Brauner et al.) to collapse to dual equivalence graphs. Key insight: the crystal already encodes the descent statistics that determine quasisymmetric expansion. This is a direct witness structure for KL left cells.

### Quasi-crystal graphs — Cain, Malheiro, F. Rodrigues, I. Rodrigues (2023/2025)
arXiv:2309.14887 and companion EJC 2025 paper. Quasi-crystal graphs for the hypoplactic monoid (the NSym/QSym analogue of the plactic monoid) are characterized with full local axioms analogous to Stembridge axioms. **Quasi-crystal graphs are the intrinsic crystal-level objects for the 0-Hecke / NSym story.** Local characterization makes them tractable for the same techniques used in KL theory.

### Weak Bruhat interval module classification — Han Yang, Houyi Yu (2024)
arXiv:2410.07990. Two H_n(0) weak Bruhat interval modules are isomorphic iff the intervals are descent-preserving isomorphic as posets. Holds for all Coxeter types. Unifies all tableau-based module families (dual immaculate, extended Schur, etc.) under one criterion. **Weak Bruhat interval modules = 0-Hecke analogue of KL cells.** This classification is the 0-Hecke counterpart to KL cell theory.

### Poset modules of H_0(S_n) and QSym — Choi, Kim, Oh (2024)
arXiv:2308.10456. ⊕_{n≥0} G_0(P(n)) ≅ QSym as Hopf algebras where P(n) = poset modules. Direct relevance to OQ2: concrete category of H_0(S_n)-modules with Grothendieck group = QSym.

### KL combinatorial invariance — Barkley-Gaetz-Esposito-Marietti (2024)
arXiv:2111.15161 + arXiv:2404.12834. Williamson et al. found ML-driven hypercube decomposition formula for P_{u,w}. Barkley-Gaetz prove parabolic combinatorial invariance conjecture is equivalent to the original. Still open but structurally approaching via hypercube decompositions.

### OPAC-023 — Westbury (2019)
Open problem: explicit descent set construction for tensor powers of arbitrary highest-weight representations, with generating quasisymmetric function recovering the character's quasisymmetric expansion. Known only for GL_n, Sp_{2n}, SO_n vector reps. Crystal operators should supply the descent function. **This is OQ1 in precise form.**

### [Crystal on Decreasing Factorizations in the 0-Hecke Monoid](https://arxiv.org/abs/1911.08732) — Morse, Pan, Poh, Schilling (2020)
The most direct bridge between 0-Hecke algebras and crystal bases: a star-crystal structure on decreasing factorizations of fully-commutative elements in H_0(S_n), intertwining under a residue map with the crystal on set-valued tableaux. The 0-Hecke monoid carries a genuine crystal structure. **This is where the combinatorial R-matrix lives in the 0-Hecke world.**

### [nLab: Quantum Yang-Baxter equation](https://ncatlab.org/nlab/show/quantum+Yang-Baxter+equation)
Confirms: the combinatorial R-matrix is the braiding isomorphism B(λ) ⊗ B(μ) → B(μ) ⊗ B(λ) in the braided monoidal category of crystals, and automatically satisfies YBE. Every quasitriangular Hopf algebra has a universal R-element. **My dream journal note is correct: the combinatorial R-matrix IS the q=0 shadow of the R-matrix, and this is forced by the braided monoidal category structure.** Notable gap: nLab has no 0-Hecke algebra page; the Hecke algebra page omits q=0, NSym, and Krob-Thibon entirely.
