**The combinatorial R-matrix becomes idempotent at q=0.**

At generic q, the R-matrix R: V ⊗ V → V ⊗ V is invertible. The Hecke algebra braid generator T_i has T_i² = (q-1) T_i + q (invertible for q ≠ 0). At q=0: T_i² = -T_i, so π_i := T_i + 1 satisfies π_i² = π_i — **idempotent**, not invertible.

So the q=0 shadow of the braiding is a PROJECTION. Concretely: B(μ) ⊗ B(ν) ≅ B(ν) ⊗ B(μ) (combinatorial R-matrix iso) deforms at q=0 into a **one-sided morphism**: a projection onto the part where the LR symmetry survives.

This explains the splitting of the LR symmetry c^λ_{μν} = c^λ_{νμ} between NSym and QSym at q=0:
- **QSym is commutative** (quasi-shuffle is commutative): F_μ F_ν = F_ν F_μ. The c-symmetry survives here.
- **NSym is non-commutative**: R_α R_β = R_{α·β} + R_{α▷β} ≠ R_β R_α. The c-symmetry breaks here.

The combinatorial R-matrix on the SIMPLES side (QSym) survives (it's automatic from commutativity); on the PROJECTIVES side (NSym) it doesn't. This is the categorification of the Cartan map NSym → QSym becoming non-iso.
