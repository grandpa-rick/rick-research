LYNDON BASES OF SPLIT ıQUANTUM GROUPS

arXiv:2502.20958v2 [math.QA] 16 Nov 2025

RUN-QIANG JIAN, LI LUO, AND XIANFA WU
Abstract. We introduce and study Lyndon bases of the split ıquantum groups
Uı (g). Especially, we provide the relation between Lyndon bases and Lusztig’s PBWtype bases of Uı (g), as well as a construction of canonical bases of Uı (g) under an
integral condition.

1. Introduction
An ıquantum group is a coideal subalgebra Uı (g) of a Drinfeld-Jimbo quantum group
U(g) such that (U(g), Uı (g)) forms a quantum symmetric pair in the sense of Letzter
[Let99, Let02]. The classification of quantum symmetric pairs (and hence ıquantum
groups) is formulated by Satake diagrams, where a Satake diagram (I = I◦ ∪ I• , τ )
consists of a bicolored partition of the Dynkin diagram I = I◦ ∪ I• and a (possibly
trivial) diagram involution τ subject to some compatibility conditions. The associated
ıquantum groups are called quasi-split if I• = ∅; and are called split if it further
satisfies τ = id. Thanks to their pioneering work [BW18a] indicating the importance
of ıquantum groups in Kazhdan-Luszitg theory, Bao and Wang suggested to generalize
fundamental constructions for quantum groups to ıquantum groups.
In the classical theory of quantum groups, there are several kinds of bases, such as
PBW-type bases [Lu93], canonical bases [Lu90], and crystal bases [Ka91]. They play
essential roles in the representation theory of quantum groups. It is a natural question
that if such kinds of bases can be constructed in the framework of ıquantum groups.
The study of canonical bases of modified ıquantum groups (named ıcanonical bases) was
initiated in [BW18a] and improved in [BW18b, BW21] by Bao and Wang. In addition,
there are completely different “dual ıcanonical bases” of universal ıquantum groups via
a quiver variety construction by Lu and Wang [LW21b]. The crystal bases of ıquantum
groups were investigated by Watanabe for some quasi-split types [Wat20, Wat24].
The first work on PBW-type bases of ıquantum groups is Iorgov and Klimyk’s investigation [IK00] for the special split ıquantum group associated with the type An Dynkin
diagram, which is the non-standard q-deformation of U (son+1 ) introduced in [GK91].
Xu and Yang [XY14] explicitly constructed PBW-type bases for all split ıquantum
groups by using the braid group actions given in [KP11]. Lu and Wang [LW22] provided PBW-type bases of quasi-split ıquantum groups associated with simply laced

On the other hand, in their work [LR95], Lalonde and Ram constructed a new type
of Gröbner basis, which are called Lyndon bases nowadays, for universal enveloping
algebras of Lie algebras in terms of good words and Lyndon words. Based on his
quantum shuffle approach to quantum groups (see [Ro98]), Rosso generalized this construction of Lyndon bases to the positive part of quantum groups in an unpublished
paper [Ro02]. As applications, he derived a multiplicative formula for the universal
R-matrix as a product of q-exponentials, and provided a new proof of the existence of
canonical bases. In [Lec04], Leclerc showed that Rosso’s Lyndon bases are proportional
to Lusztig’s PBW-type bases obtained by braid group actions, and used this fact to
provide another proof of the existence of dual canonical bases. A direct shuffle construction of dual canonical bases (in a super generalization) is due to Clark, Hill and
Wang [CHW16].
Contrary to those constructions of bases mentioned before, Lyndon bases are much
easier to compute. However, there has been no work to give bases for ıquantum groups
by using good Lyndon words yet. In this paper, we aim at generalizing the construction
in [LR95] to split ıquantum groups. We introduce good words for any split ıquantum
group Uı (g) in the sense of [LR95], and give the good Lyndon words by which we can
construct a Lyndon basis of Uı (g) (see Theorem 4.1.5). Furthermore, we provide a relationship between the Lyndon basis and Xu-Yang’s PBW-type basis in Theorem 4.2.4,
which says that each Lyndon basis element is the leading term of a PBW-type basis
element in some sense. In particular, we prove in Corollary 4.3.2 that the Lyndon basis
coincides with Xu-Yang’s PBW-type basis for the type A split ıquantum group Uı (sln ).
These Lyndon bases enable us to construct canonical bases for Uı (g), as Rosso [Ro02]
and Leclerc [Lec04] did for quantum groups. However, their approach does not work
in our case due to the lack of quantum shuffle realization of ıquantum groups at this
stage. Instead, we introduce an integral condition by which a canonical basis can be
produced (Theorem 5.1.12). As an application, in Theorem 5.2.3 we show that the
split ıquantum groups Uı (sln ) satisfy this integral condition and hence afford canonical
bases. We believe that the integral condition would have further interests; but on the
other hand, we must also acknowledge that it is not always valid (see Example 5.1.6
for type G2 ).
The paper is organized as follows. In Section 2, we recall the basic definitions of
quantum groups and split ıquantum groups. In Section 3, we introduce good Lyndon words and study some of their properties. We construct Lyndon bases for split
ıquantum groups in Section 4. We also study the relationship between Lyndon bases
and PBW-type bases. Finally, we establish canonical bases for the split ıquantum
groups Uı (sln ) in Section 5.

3. Lyndon words and good words
In this section, we will introduce the notion of Uı -good word. We will always assume
that V is a vector space over Q(q) with a preferred basis v1 , v2 , . . . , vn . For simplicity,
we always write a pure tensor vi1 ⊗ · · · ⊗ vik in T (V ) as vi1 · · · vik . We call the basis
elements vi letters, and call a pure tensor in T (V ) a word. The set of words is denote by
W. The set including all words and the empty word is denoted by W∗ . Therefore, W∗ is
a basis of T (V ). For a word w = vi1 · · · vik , the number k is called the length of w, and is
denoted by ℓ(w). We assign each word x = vi1 · · · vik an NΠ-degree |x| = αi1 + · · · + αik .
Given ν ∈ Q(q), the ν-commutator [·, ·]ν on T (V ) is defined to be
[x, y]ν = xy − ν (|x|,|y|) yx,
for any x, y ∈ W.
3.1. Lyndon words. We endow the set W with the following lexicographic order <:
(1) v1 < v2 < · · · < vn ;
(2) for any x, y ∈ W, x < y if either y = xz for some z ∈ W of length ≥ 1, or
x = uvi w1 and y = uvj w2 with vi < vj and u, w1 , w2 ∈ W∗ .
Definition 3.1.1. A word l = vi1 · · · vik is called a Lyndon word if either ℓ(l) = 1 or
l < vij vij+1 · · · vik

for all j = 2, . . . , k.

3.2. Good words. Following [LR95], we define another total order ⪯ on W as follows.
For any x, y ∈ W, x ≺ y if either the length of x is strictly less than that of y, or they
have the same length and x > y. Notice that there are only finitely many words less
than a given word with respect to this order. So (W, ≺) is well ordered. We can apply
the well-founded induction to (W, ≺).
The following properties of the order ≺ are useful in our study of Lyndon bases.

Proof. Observe that if ℓ(x) < ℓ(y), then ℓ(wxz) < ℓ(wyz).
