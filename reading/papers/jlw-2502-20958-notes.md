## 1. Scope of the paper

JLW construct **Lyndon bases** of split iquantum groups $\mathbf{U}^\imath(\mathfrak{g})$
(split Satake type, $I_\bullet = \emptyset$, $\tau = \mathrm{id}$).

- **Main theorem 4.1.5:** For each split $\mathbf{U}^\imath$, two related sets are bases:
  - $\mathcal{B}_{GL} = \{B_{l_1 l_2 \cdots l_m} : l_i \in \mathrm{GL}(\mathbf{U}^\imath),\; l_1 \geq \cdots \geq l_m\}$
    (good Lyndon word PBW-style products).
  - $\mathcal{B}_L = \{B_{[l_1][l_2]\cdots[l_m]} : l_i \in \mathrm{GL}(\mathbf{U}^\imath),\; l_1 \geq \cdots \geq l_m\}$
    (q^{-1}-bracketed analogues). $\mathcal{B}_L$ is called the **Lyndon basis**.
- Construction follows Lalonde-Ram / Leclerc / Rosso. $\mathbf{U}^\imath$-good = $\mathbf{U}^+$-good
  (Prop 3.2.4). So the set of good Lyndon words $\mathrm{GL}(\mathbf{U}^\imath)$
  matches the standard set of good Lyndon words for $\mathbf{U}^+(\mathfrak{g})$,
  taken from Leclerc [Lec04].

## 2. Example 5.1.5 (Type B_2) — verbatim summary

**Setup.** Cartan convention: $(\alpha_1, \alpha_1) = 4$, $(\alpha_2, \alpha_2) = 2$,
$(\alpha_1, \alpha_2) = -1$. So $\alpha_1$ is **long**, $\alpha_2$ is **short**.
Dynkin arrow convention is OPPOSITE to [Lec04, 8.2].

Cartan integer $a_{12} = 2(\alpha_1, \alpha_2) / (\alpha_2, \alpha_2) = -1$;
$a_{21} = 2(\alpha_1, \alpha_2) / (\alpha_1, \alpha_1) = -1/2$ — wait,
that's wrong. Let me re-derive: $a_{ij} = 2(\alpha_i, \alpha_j)/(\alpha_i, \alpha_i)$.
$a_{12} = 2(-1)/4 = -1/2$? No — JLW must mean $(\alpha_i, \alpha_j)$ in their
convention is not normalised the standard way. Looking at the Serre relations they
write:
$$B_1^2 B_2 - [2]_{q^2} B_1 B_2 B_1 + B_2 B_1^2 = B_2,$$
$$B_2^3 B_1 - [3] B_2^2 B_1 B_2 + [3] B_2 B_1 B_2^2 - B_1 B_2^3 = [2]^2(B_2 B_1 - B_1 B_2).$$
The first is the iSerre of $a_{12} = -1$ (quadratic in $B_1$), the second is
the iSerre of $a_{21} = -2$ (cubic in $B_2$). So in **JLW's labelling**:
- $\alpha_1$ = long, $\alpha_2$ = short.
- $a_{21} = -2$ (i.e., row-$2$ col-$1$ Cartan entry; the row that's cubic).
- The short simple is $B_2$ (= the one with cubic Serre involving $B_1$).

**Good Lyndon words (B_2):** $v_1, v_1 v_2, v_1 v_2 v_2, v_2$ (four words),
of multidegrees $\alpha_1, \alpha_1+\alpha_2, \alpha_1+2\alpha_2, \alpha_2$.
All good words are $v_2^{k_1} (v_1 v_2 v_2)^{k_2} (v_1 v_2)^{k_3} v_1^{k_4}$.

**Lyndon basis elements at B_2** (Ex 4.1.9, q^{-1}-bracket):
| Element | Expression | Multidegree | Bracket structure |
|---|---|---|---|
| $B_{[v_1]}$ | $B_{v_1}$ | $\alpha_1$ (long) | trivial |
| $B_{[v_2]}$ | $B_{v_2}$ | $\alpha_2$ (short) | trivial |
| $B_{[v_1 v_2]}$ | $B_{v_1 v_2} - q^2 B_{v_2 v_1}$ | $\alpha_1 + \alpha_2$ | $[B_1, B_2]_{q^{-1}}$ across short-long edge |
| $B_{[v_1 v_2 v_2]}$ | $B_{v_1 v_2 v_2} - (q^2+1) B_{v_2 v_1 v_2} + q^2 B_{v_2 v_2 v_1}$ | $\alpha_1 + 2\alpha_2$ | nested commutator $[B_1, [B_2, B_2]_{q^{-1}}]_{q^{-1}}$ |

Per Prop 3.1.2 (PBW theorem on Lyndon factorisations), the **Lyndon basis of
$\mathbf{U}^\imath(\mathfrak{so}_5)$** is the set of all PBW-type ordered monomials
in these four generators, with weak-decreasing order
$v_2 \leq v_1 v_2 v_2 \leq v_1 v_2 \leq v_1$:
$$\{ B_{[v_2]}^{k_1} B_{[v_1 v_2 v_2]}^{k_2} B_{[v_1 v_2]}^{k_3} B_{[v_1]}^{k_4} : k_i \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0} \}.$$

So as a **set of basis elements at the algebra level**, the Lyndon basis is *infinite*
(PBW on 4 generators), but it's generated by **4 root vectors**, in 1-1 correspondence
with positive roots $\Phi^+(B_2) = \{\alpha_1, \alpha_1+\alpha_2, \alpha_1+2\alpha_2, \alpha_2\}$
of cardinality $|\Phi^+(B_2)| = 4$. This is the iquantum analogue of Lusztig's PBW basis
$F_{\beta_1}^{a_1} \cdots F_{\beta_N}^{a_N}$ of $\mathbf{U}^-$.

**Example 5.1.5 content** beyond the basis: JLW verify the integral condition (IC)
at $B_2$ by computing 4 straightening identities:
- $B_{v_1 \cdot v_1 v_2}$ in terms of $B_{[w]}$'s.
- $B_{v_1 \cdot v_1 v_2 v_2}$.
- $B_{(v_1 v_2) \cdot (v_1 v_2 v_2)}$.
- $B_{(v_1 v_2 v_2) \cdot v_2}$.

These are the 4 "X-pairs" — adjacent pairs $(l, l')$ of good Lyndon words with $l < l'$
where $ll'$ is not itself a good word; they index the Serre-style straightening relations.

### What IS the relationship (framework-level)?

JLW Lyndon basis lives at the **algebra** $\mathbf{U}^\imath$ level. Rick's catalog
lives at the **crystal** $K_p(\infty)$-action level. The connection between these
two levels is the standard "PBW basis at $q=1$ degenerates to a monomial basis on
the symmetric algebra of $\mathfrak{n}^-$" picture, BUT in the iquantum case the
correspondence is mediated by the $\imath$Hall algebra (Lu-Wang) or the iSerre
nested commutators (CLW). Neither of these gives a catalog-class-to-Lyndon-element
bijection — the right way to view JLW is as the **algebra-level companion** to
Rick's combinatorial structure, not a bijective shadow.

**Framework-level observation worth noting:** The JLW B_2 Lyndon basis element
$B_{[v_1 v_2 v_2]} = B_{v_1 v_2 v_2} - (q^2+1) B_{v_2 v_1 v_2} + q^2 B_{v_2 v_2 v_1}$
is the unique generator that picks up a non-trivial "doubled" structure (coefficient
$[3]_{q^2}$-like patterns). This is the algebra-level reflection of the same
"doubled interior brackets" $(^{2B} \, )^{2T}$ that Rick's chain factor $\mathcal{C}_a$
exhibits at the bracket level. So there IS a framework-level commonality: both
encode $a_{n-1,n} = -2$ via a structure of multiplicity 2/3 at the same Chevalley
position. But it's a *texture* similarity, not a count bijection.

### If the bridge were to be salvaged (ambiguity resolver)

The single computation that would tell us anything: compute the JLW Lyndon basis at
$B_3$ and check whether the 9 good Lyndon words split into classes that match
Rick's $6 + 4 + 5 = 15$ in any natural way. Per JLW Ex 5.1.5 logic, $B_3$ would
have $|\Phi^+(B_3)| = 9$ good Lyndon words; if any natural pairing structure (X-pairs
or quadratic relations) gives 15 with a 6/4/5 split, the bridge is back on the table.
**Prediction (calibrated):** It won't. The X-pair count at $B_3$ is determined by
adjacency in the lex order on good Lyndon words and has no reason to factor as 6+4+5.
