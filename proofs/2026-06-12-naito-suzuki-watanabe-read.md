## 1. NSW main theorem (precise)

### 1a. The Naito–Sagaki conjecture (Theorem 3.2 / Theorem A)

Let $\mathfrak{g} = \mathfrak{gl}_{2n}(\mathbb{C})$, $I = \{1, \ldots, 2n-1\}$
vertices of Dynkin $A_{2n-1}$. Diagram automorphism $\sigma : i \mapsto 2n - i$
acts on $\mathfrak{sl}_{2n}$; fixed-point subalgebra $\hat{\mathfrak{g}}
:= \mathfrak{sl}_{2n}^\sigma \cong \mathfrak{sp}_{2n}(\mathbb{C})$ with
Dynkin diagram $C_n$ and Chevalley generators

$$
\hat{e}_i = e_i + e_{\sigma(i)},\ \hat{f}_i = f_i + f_{\sigma(i)},\
\hat{h}_i = h_i + h_{\sigma(i)} \quad (1 \le i < n);\
\hat{e}_n = e_n,\ \hat{f}_n = f_n,\ \hat{h}_n = h_n.
$$

For $\lambda \in P^+_{\ge 0} \cong \mathrm{Par}_{\le 2n}$ and $T \in
\mathrm{SST}_{2n}(\lambda)$, define a piecewise-linear path
$\pi_T : [0,1] \to P_{\mathbb{R}}$ from the inverse column word
$w_c(T) = i_1 i_2 \cdots i_N$ ($N = |\lambda|$) by

$$
\pi_T(t) = \sum_{k<j} \varepsilon_{i_k} + N(t - t_{j-1}) \varepsilon_{i_j},\quad
t_{j-1} \le t \le t_j,\quad t_j = j/N.
$$

Restrict to $\hat\pi_T : [0,1] \to \hat P_{\mathbb{R}}$ via $\hat\varepsilon_i
= -\hat\varepsilon_{\bar i}$ where $\bar i = 2n - i + 1$. Call $T$
**$\hat{\mathfrak{g}}$-dominant** iff $\langle\hat\pi_T(t),\hat h_i\rangle
\ge 0$ for all $i \in \hat I$ and $t \in [0,1]$. Let
$\mathrm{SST}^{\hat{\mathfrak{g}}\text{-dom}}_{2n}(\lambda)$ denote this
subset.

**Theorem 3.2 (NS conjecture).** For all $\mu \in \hat P^+$,

$$
\Big[\mathrm{Res}^{\mathfrak{g}}_{\hat{\mathfrak{g}}} L(\lambda) : \hat L(\mu)\Big]
= \#\{T \in \mathrm{SST}^{\hat{\mathfrak{g}}\text{-dom}}_{2n}(\lambda)
: \mathrm{wt}_{\hat{\mathfrak{g}}}(T) = \mu\}.
$$

### 1b. The combinatorial reformulation (Theorem 5.18 / Theorem B)

NSW prove Theorem 3.2 by establishing the bijection layer:

$$
\Phi : \mathrm{SST}^{\hat{\mathfrak{g}}\text{-dom}}_{2n}(\lambda)
\xrightarrow{\sim} \mathrm{SST}^{\mathfrak{k}\text{-hw}}_{2n}(\lambda),\quad
\Psi : \mathrm{SST}^{\hat{\mathfrak{g}}\text{-dom}}_{2n}(\lambda)
\xrightarrow{\sim} \mathrm{SST}^{\mathfrak{k}\text{-lw}}_{2n}(\lambda),
$$

intertwining weight under restriction: $\mathrm{wt}_{\hat{\mathfrak{g}}}(T)
= \mathrm{wt}_{\mathfrak{k}}(\Phi(T)) = \mathrm{wt}_{\mathfrak{k}}(\Psi(T))$.
Here $\mathfrak{k}$ is the "iquantum-fixed Lie subalgebra of $\mathfrak{g}$"
acting via specific $X_i, Y_i, Z_i$ generators (NSW §5.1), with
$\mathfrak{k} \cong \mathfrak{sp}_{2n}$ as well; Dixmier
(Proposition 5.17) plus Watanabe 2023 (Theorem 5.1) reduce Theorem A to
Theorem B.

## 2. The iquantum-crystal mechanics (5 bullets)

**(M1) ıquantum group $U^\imath$.** The C(q)-subalgebra of $U_q(\mathfrak{sl}_{2n})$
generated by $K_{h_i}, E_i, F_i$ for $i$ odd ($I_\bullet$) and by

$$
B_j := F_j - q[E_{j-1}, [E_{j+1}, E_j]_{q^{-1}}]_{q^{-1}} K_{h_j}^{-1},\quad
A_j := -q^{-1}[F_{j-1}, [F_{j+1}, B_j]_{q}]_{q}
$$

for $j$ even ($I_\circ$). This is a coideal subalgebra, $q$-deforming
$U(\mathfrak{k})$. The "ı-Chevalley generators" $X_i, Y_i, Z_i$
($1 \le i \le n$) are explicit $A_{2i}, B_{2i}, F_{2n-1}, E_{2n-1}$
combinations with parity-dependent signs (NSW Eqs. above 5.4).

**(M2) Successor / record map.** For $S \in \mathrm{SST}_{2n}$ with
first column $S_1$, the **remove** set $\mathrm{rem}(S_1)$ is defined
recursively by stripping matched pairs $(S(1,l-1), S(1,l)) = (j, j+1)$
with $j+1$ even and $j+1 < 2l - \#\mathrm{rem}(S'') - 1$. Then
$\mathrm{red}(S_1)$ is $S_1$ with those entries removed, and the
**successor** is $\mathrm{suc}(S) = \mathrm{red}(S_1) * S_{\ge 2}$
(Schensted column-insertion of $\mathrm{red}(S_1)$ into the rest of $S$).
Iterating to a fixed point yields $P_{\mathrm{AII}}(S)$ (always symplectic);
the recording tableau $Q_{\mathrm{AII}}(S)$ tracks shape decrements.

**(M3) The iquantum LR bijection.** (Watanabe 2023, Theorem 5.10
in NSW): For $\lambda \in \mathrm{Par}_{\le 2n}$,

$$
\mathrm{LR}_{\mathrm{AII}} : \mathrm{SST}_{2n}(\lambda)
\xrightarrow{\sim} \bigsqcup_{\mu \in \mathrm{Par}_{\le n}}
\mathrm{SpT}_{2n}(\mu) \times \mathrm{Rec}_{2n}(\lambda/\mu),
\quad
S \mapsto (P_{\mathrm{AII}}(S), Q_{\mathrm{AII}}(S)).
$$

This is the iquantum analogue of classical RSK; "melted" to q-level it
gives $\mathrm{Res}^U_{U^\imath} L_q(\lambda) \cong \bigoplus_\mu
\widetilde L^\imath(\mu) \otimes \mathbb{C}(q)\mathrm{Rec}_{2n}(\lambda/\mu)$.

**(M4) Restriction / promotion operators.** For $1 \le a < b \le 2n$,
$\mathrm{pr}_{a,b}$ is a *promotion* operator on partial tableaux
(NSW Def 6.4 et seq), and $\mathrm{Res}_{a,b;c,d}(T) := \mathrm{Rect}(T|_{a,b;c,d})$
extracts and rectifies the subtableau with entries in $[a,b] \cup [c,d]$
via jeu-de-taquin slides through a "marked" cell $\bullet$. These
satisfy $\mathrm{pr}_{a,a+1}^2 = \mathrm{id}$, $\mathrm{pr}_{a,b}$ and
$\mathrm{pr}_{c,d}$ commute when $|b-c|\ge 2$, and $\mathrm{pr}_{a,b}
\circ \mathrm{pr}_{b,c} = \mathrm{pr}_{a,c}$ (Lemma 7.7).

**(M5) Local-to-global reduction.** The proof of Theorem 5.18 for
$n \ge 3$ reduces to the $n = 2$ case ($\mathfrak{sp}_4$) by localising
through restriction operators $\mathrm{Res}_{i,i+1;\overline{i+1},\bar i}$,
which preserve $\hat{\mathfrak{g}}_i$-dominance (Lemma 7.10), and gluing
back via promotion operators. The bijection $\Phi$ is built as an
explicit composite of $\mathrm{pr}_{a,b}$ across the localization
indices.

## 3. AII → BDI translation sketch

Recall the **structural twin question**: NSW work at the Cartan-Lie-algebra
symmetric pair $(\mathfrak{sl}_{2n}, \mathfrak{sp}_{2n})$ (AII). Rick's
project-internal "BDI" target is the orthogonal twin in the
symmetric-pair landscape, specifically the iquantum group $U^\imath_{\mathrm{BDI}}$
with target Lie algebra $\mathfrak{so}_{2n+1}$ (= type $B_n$), already
the subject of Watanabe-style AI-crystal precedent (arXiv:2107.00170)
at simply-laced AI = $\mathfrak{sl}_n \supset \mathfrak{so}_n$. The
**double-rank** version $\mathfrak{sl}_{2n} \supset \mathfrak{so}_{2n}$
(type $D_n$ orthogonal) is the strict analog of NSW's
$\mathfrak{sl}_{2n} \supset \mathfrak{sp}_{2n}$.

| Slot | NSW (AII → CI) | Rick (AII → BDI) candidate |
|---|---|---|
| Symmetric pair (Lie algebra) | $(\mathfrak{sl}_{2n}, \mathfrak{sp}_{2n})$ | $(\mathfrak{sl}_{2n}, \mathfrak{so}_{2n})$ [strict] or $(\mathfrak{so}_{2n+1}, \mathfrak{so}_{2p+1} \oplus \mathfrak{so}_{2(n-p)})$ [Rick's chain-factor home] |
| Satake colouring | $I_\bullet = $ odd nodes of $A_{2n-1}$ | type AI: $I_\bullet = \emptyset$, $\sigma = \mathrm{id}$ on $A_{n-1}$ |
| Fixed subalgebra $\mathfrak{k}$ via $B_j, A_j$ | $\mathfrak{sp}_{2n}$, $C_n$ Dynkin | $\mathfrak{so}_n$, type $B_{(n-1)/2}$ or $D_{n/2}$ |
| Combinatorial input | $\mathrm{SST}_{2n}(\lambda)$, KN tableaux | $\mathrm{SST}_{2n}^{\mathrm{AI}}(\lambda) \subset \mathrm{SST}_{2n}$ (Watanabe's AI-condition: column-length bound + first-column inequality) |
| Successor / iquantum-LR | $\mathrm{suc}$ via rem on first column (pairs $j, j+1$ with $j+1$ even) | Rick's chain-factor descent (Day 22 prove session): step types {chain-top, chain-mid, chain-bot, singleton-shift} on the rightmost surviving $)$ |
| $P,Q$ output | $(P_{\mathrm{AII}}, Q_{\mathrm{AII}}) \in \mathrm{SpT}_{2n}(\mu) \times \mathrm{Rec}_{2n}(\lambda/\mu)$ | $(P_{\mathrm{BDI}}, Q_{\mathrm{BDI}}) \in \mathrm{OrthT}_{2n}(\mu) \times \mathrm{Rec}^{\mathrm{BDI}}_{2n}(\lambda/\mu)$ where $\mathrm{OrthT}$ = King–Welsh orthogonal tableaux |
| Symplectic ↔ branching | Sundaram 1990 / Kwon 2018 / Schumann–Torres 2018 | Sundaram's *orthogonal* branching model (1990) / Kumar–Torres 2412.19721 / Muniz 2505.21738 analogue |
| Crystal stratification | $\imath$-crystal components = $\mathfrak{sp}_{2n}$-irrep multiplicities | $\imath$-crystal components candidate = $\mathfrak{so}_{2n}$-irrep multiplicities |
| Relative Weyl group | $W(C_n)$ | $W(B_{\lfloor n/2 \rfloor})$ or $W(D_{n/2})$ depending on parity |
| $w_0 = -1$? | YES (all $n$) | YES for $B_k, D_{2k}$; NO for $D_{2k+1}$ |

**Key translation gaps:**
1. The "rem" rule of NSW (M2) is tuned to the AII colouring (pair entries
  $j, j+1$ at even $j+1$). The orthogonal analog should pair
  $(j, \bar j)$ where $\bar j = 2n + 1 - j$ — i.e., complementary indices
  rather than adjacent ones. This is the **chain-factor** signature of
  Rick's BDIqLR, and matches the AI-crystal "first-column inequality"
  of Watanabe.
2. Promotion operators $\mathrm{pr}_{a,b}$ for AII commute when adjacent
  index gap $\ge 2$. For orthogonal BDI the analog should respect the
  reflection $\bar{\cdot}$, giving a $\mathbb{Z}/2$-twisted promotion.
  Rick's "on-slice = squared off-slice" calibration (Theorem A,
  Day-22 prove session) is the right structural primitive.
3. The local-global reduction in NSW reduces $n \ge 3$ to $n = 2$
  ($\mathfrak{sp}_4$). The BDI analog should reduce to $\mathfrak{so}_4
  \cong \mathfrak{sl}_2 \oplus \mathfrak{sl}_2$ (a *split* Lie algebra,
  not simple). This is structurally **easier than NSW's $\mathfrak{sp}_4$
  base case** because $\mathfrak{so}_4$ splits. The base case of Rick's
  BDIqLR at $B_2$ confirms saturation.

## 4. Crystal-level marginal-palindromy check

### 4a. Setup

The Day-64 filter (`memory/connections/marginal-palindromy-refutation.md`):
for any $\mathfrak{g}$ with $w_0 = -1$, every linear functional projection
of $\mathrm{wt}(V)$ has palindromic histogram. Bucket-2's axis
marginals are non-palindromic on $i_2$ ($\{1, 2, 9, 10\}$), $i_{236}$
($\{1,1,1,1,2,3,3,4,6\}$), $i_{23456}$ ($\{1,1,1,2,4,4,4,5\}$). $B_3$
(axis $\{5,5,12\}$) and $C_3$ (axis $\{1,1,4,4,12\}$) refuted.

**Question:** does $\imath$-crystal structure (not bare weight character)
escape this obstruction?

### 4b. The answer is NO at the q=1 level

The crystal $B(\lambda)$ at $q=1$ specialises to the weight character of
$L_q(\lambda)$; the multiset of weights equals $\mathrm{wt}(L(\lambda))$.
For NSW's iquantum-crystal: the components of $B(\lambda)$ under the
$\imath$-Kashiwara operators are in bijection with the $\mathfrak{sp}_{2n}$-irreducible
constituents of $\mathrm{Res}^{\mathfrak{g}}_{\mathfrak{k}} L(\lambda)$.
Each component carries the weight character of its $\mathfrak{sp}_{2n}$-irrep
(in $\widetilde P$ valued in relative root lattice). $\mathfrak{sp}_{2n}$ has
$w_0 = -1$ (type $C_n$, all $n$). Hence **the $\imath$-crystal-component
weight multiset is palindromic on every axis under the same filter.**

**Consequence:** if Bucket-2 (or its MAX-stratum-vector) is to arise as
the $\mathfrak{k}$-irrep multiplicity decomposition of any restriction
$\mathrm{Res}^{\mathfrak{g}}_{\mathfrak{k}} L(\lambda)$ with $w_0(W_K) = -1$,
its axis marginals MUST be palindromic. They are not. **OBSTRUCTION
INHERITED at $\imath$-crystal level.**

### 4c. The genuine loophole: $D_3 = A_3$ (and $D_{2k+1}$ in general)

The filter is **silent** for the orthogonal-branching cases where
$w_0 \ne -1$ on the target weight lattice:

| $\mathfrak{k}$ candidate at $n = 3$ | Cartan type | $w_0 = -1$? | Filter status |
|---|---|---|---|
| $\mathfrak{sp}_6$ | $C_3$ | YES | Bucket-2 RULED OUT (Day 64) |
| $\mathfrak{so}_7$ | $B_3$ | YES | Bucket-2 RULED OUT (Day 64) |
| $\mathfrak{so}_6$ | $D_3 \cong A_3$ | **NO** | **OPEN: filter inapplicable** |
| $\mathfrak{so}_5 \cong \mathfrak{sp}_4$ | $B_2 = C_2$ | YES | rank too low for ambient $[4]\times[9]\times[8]$ |

The dimension-22 Bucket-2 sub-configuration COULD in principle arise
from a $\mathfrak{so}_6$-branching:
$\mathfrak{sl}_6 \supset \mathfrak{so}_6$ has classical decomposition
into King–Welsh orthogonal-tableaux components, and $\mathfrak{so}_6$
is the *only* small-rank target where the $w_0 = -1$ obstruction does
not apply. This is the strict analog of NSW's $\mathfrak{sl}_{2n}
\supset \mathfrak{sp}_{2n}$ with $n = 3$: $\mathfrak{sl}_6 \supset
\mathfrak{so}_6$, type-AI symmetric pair.

**Caveat 1.** $D_3 \cong A_3$ has $w_0 \ne -1$ on the full weight
lattice but $w_0$ acts as the *diagram involution* (swap of the two
nodes of $D_3$ that are spinor-related). So the *multiset* of weights
is still invariant under $\mu \mapsto -w_0(\mu) \ne -\mu$. This means:
axis marginal palindromy is replaced by a **diagram-twisted palindromy**
on the spinor-pair-coordinate. The filter must be reformulated as:
$\{\mu_i\} = \{-\mu_{\sigma_0(i)}\}$ where $\sigma_0$ is the diagram
involution. For $D_3 = A_3$ on $\varepsilon_1, \varepsilon_2, \varepsilon_3$
coordinates, $\sigma_0$ is the swap $1 \leftrightarrow 3$ (fixing 2).
**Re-test Bucket-2 axis marginals against this twisted condition.**

**Caveat 2.** For $n = 4$ → $D_4$: $w_0 = -1$, filter applies, obstruction
returns. **OQ-NAITOSAGAKI-BDI cannot survive to all $n$ at type-$D$**
under the iquantum-crystal description; at most odd-rank-$D$ targets
work, and even those only at the specific MAX-vector entries that
survive twisted palindromy.

### 4d. Crystal-graph escape: the stratification angle

The MAX-stratum-vector $(3, 8, 11, 10, 19, 14, 23, 26)$ has 8 entries —
one per coordinate direction $\sigma \in \{0,1\}^3$. Each entry counts
*distinct $\sigma$-active column profiles among 26 pieces*. This is
NOT an irrep-multiplicity vector. It is a **stratification invariant**
that records, projection-by-projection, the support of the piece registry.

The NSW iquantum-crystal bijection gives a *single* count
$|\mathrm{SST}^{\hat{\mathfrak{g}}\text{-dom}}_{2n}(\lambda)|$ per
$(\lambda, \mu)$. Not 8 distinct counts. The bridge AII → BDI as
formulated cannot deliver the 8-vector directly.

**But:** NSW's restriction operators $\mathrm{Res}_{a,b;c,d}$ ARE
indexed by intervals $(a,b;c,d)$, and the proof uses 8-ish distinct
choices in the $n = 3$ case. Plausible reformulation: each MAX-vector
entry $v_\sigma$ is the cardinality of the image of a distinct
restriction operator under a $\sigma$-prescribed marking. **This is a
testable structural prediction.** See §5.

## 5. Concrete computational test (CODE prescription for Day 66)

### 5a. Test 1 — King–Welsh orthogonal branching at $\mathfrak{sl}_6 \supset \mathfrak{so}_6$

Compute, for each partition $\lambda \subset (4^3)$ or
$\lambda \subset (5^3)$ with $\ell(\lambda) \le 6$ and $|\lambda| \le 22$:

1. $\mathrm{Res}^{\mathfrak{sl}_6}_{\mathfrak{so}_6} L(\lambda)$ as a
   multi-set of $\mathfrak{so}_6$-irrep highest weights using Sundaram's
   orthogonal branching algorithm.
2. The **axis-marginal multiset** of the underlying weight multiset.
3. **Twisted palindromy test:** is the multiset invariant under
   $\mu \mapsto -w_0(\mu)$ where $w_0$ is the longest element of
   $W(D_3) = S_4$? Expect YES on every axis (this is forced by
   irreducibility).
4. Compare every prefix-of-axis projection $(\bar i, i_{23}, i_{236},
   \ldots, i_{23456}, \mathrm{all})$ to Bucket-2 marginals
   ($\{1,2,9,10\}$ etc).

**Pass/fail criterion:** if no $\lambda$ gives an exact match to
Bucket-2 marginals on at least one prefix-axis, OQ-NAITOSAGAKI-BDI
is definitively refuted. If a match is found, that $\lambda$ is the
candidate "host" rep and the 26-piece registry is its $\mathfrak{so}_6$-branching
image.

**Estimated cost:** $|\{\lambda \subset (5^3) : |\lambda| \le 22\}|
\approx 50$ candidates × ~10 ms each for branching computation in
SageMath. Total < 5 minutes.

### 5b. Test 2 — restriction-operator stratification map

For each $\sigma \in \{0,1\}^3$ (8 directions):

1. Identify the NSW restriction operator $\mathrm{Res}_{a_\sigma,b_\sigma;c_\sigma,d_\sigma}$
   whose interval marking matches the $\sigma$-stratum direction.
2. Compute $|\mathrm{Res}_{a_\sigma,b_\sigma;c_\sigma,d_\sigma}(\mathrm{SST}^{\hat g\text{-dom}}_6(\lambda))|$
   for $\lambda$ the candidate from Test 1.
3. Compare the resulting 8-vector to $(3, 8, 11, 10, 19, 14, 23, 26)$.

**Pass/fail criterion:** exact match = OQ-NAITOSAGAKI-BDI **proved**
(structurally — Bucket-2 is then a $D_3$-iquantum-crystal stratification).
Mismatch on any entry = the strata are finer than NSW restrictions can
resolve; need either a stronger iquantum machinery or accept that the
8-vector is genuinely intrinsic (Day-64 closed-negative interpretation
stands).

### 5c. Test 3 — twisted-palindromy retrocheck (RAN IN-SESSION)

**Result (Day-65 in-session):** every axis-level-multiplicity sequence of
Bucket-2 is **non-palindromic** under simple level reversal $k \to L-1-k$:

| Axis | Level-multiplicity sequence | Palindromic? |
|---|---|---|
| $i_2$ (length 4) | $[9, 10, 2, 1]$ | NO |
| $i_{236}$ (length 9) | $[6, 3, 4, 3, 2, 1, 1, 1, 1]$ | NO |
| $i_{23456}$ (length 8) | $[1, 4, 1, 2, 4, 5, 4, 1]$ | NO |

Simultaneous sign-flip (level reversal) on any nontrivial subset of axes:
**no choice gives multiset invariance.** Only the identity flip preserves
the 22-triple multiset.

Axis-length asymmetry $(4, 9, 8)$ ALL DISTINCT precludes any axis
permutation acting as a symmetry; combined with the failure of all
sign-flip subsets, this means **no rank-3 Weyl-group element of any
ABCD-type can act as a discrete symmetry on the Bucket-2 axis structure
*as embedded***.

**However:** the $D_3$ Weyl group $W(D_3) = S_4$ acts on $\mathbb{R}^3$
as **signed permutations with an even number of sign changes**. A
*choice of linear basis change* $A : \mathbb{R}^3 \to \mathbb{R}^3$
might still map Bucket-2 into a $W(D_3)$-stable configuration. So this
retrocheck does NOT rule out $\mathfrak{so}_6$-branching as a host for
Bucket-2; Test 1 (§5a, SageMath) remains required.

**Refined palindromy filter** (banked as a v2 of `marginal-palindromy-refutation.md`):
> For types with $w_0 \ne -1$ (notably $A_n$, $D_{2k+1}$, $E_6$), the
> axis-marginal palindromy test is not directly applicable; instead,
> test invariance under the explicit Weyl-group element representation
> on $\mathbb{R}^{\mathrm{rank}}$ via signed permutations / diagram
> involution. For Bucket-2 axes of distinct lengths, only basis-change
> realisations are admissible — full SageMath enumeration of
> branching multiplicities is the natural decisive test.

## 6. Verdict on OQ-NAITOSAGAKI-BDI

**Status: PARTIALLY REFUTED, NARROW LOOPHOLE OPEN.**

1. The marginal-palindromy obstruction (Day 64) DOES propagate to the
   $\imath$-crystal level under the NSW mechanism, because $\imath$-crystal
   components carry $\mathfrak{k}$-irrep weight characters and $\mathfrak{k}$
   inherits Weyl symmetry from its Cartan type.

2. **Symplectic / odd-orthogonal targets** ($\mathfrak{sp}_{2n}, \mathfrak{so}_{2n+1}$)
   are RULED OUT for all $n$ because $C_n$ and $B_n$ have $w_0 = -1$.

3. **Even-orthogonal-low-rank** target $\mathfrak{so}_6$ ($D_3 = A_3$,
   $w_0 \ne -1$) is the **single surviving loophole** at the
   $n = 3$ scale of the Bucket-2 problem.

4. At $n = 4$ and higher, $D_n$-targets fall under $w_0 = -1$ when
   $n$ even, so the loophole closes structurally beyond $n = 3$.

5. **The MAX-stratum-vector cannot be a single irrep-multiplicity vector**
   regardless: it is 8 distinct counts, not one count. The bridge to
   NSW machinery must invoke 8 distinct restriction operators
   simultaneously, which is a finer structure than NSW's
   irrep-decomposition output.

6. Consequently: even if Test 1 (§5a) finds an $\mathfrak{so}_6$ host,
   recovering the full 8-vector requires Test 2 (§5b) to also succeed.
   These are stacked tests. Either failure refutes the conjecture.

**Probability estimate (subjective, Rick's gut):** ~15-20% chance Test 1
finds an $\mathfrak{so}_6$ host with matching marginals; ~5% conditional
on that, that Test 2's 8-vector also matches. Joint ≤ 1%. **OQ-NAITOSAGAKI-BDI
is mostly closed-negative, with one narrow $\mathfrak{so}_6$ check still
to fire in Day 66 CODE.**
