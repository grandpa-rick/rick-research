## Headline

**The tensor product rule across the short-long edge of $B_n$.** Restricted
to the action of $B_n$ (the short simple), the Kostant-partition crystal
factors as
$$
\mathrm{Kp}(\infty)\big|_{B_n\text{-action}}
\;\cong\;
\bigotimes_{a=1}^{n-1} \mathcal{C}_a \;\otimes\; \mathcal{C}_{\mathrm{sing}}
$$
as ι-crystals, where the **chain factor** $\mathcal{C}_a$ has a four-block
internal bracketing
$$
\mathrm{br}(\mathcal{C}_a)(M,B,T) \;=\; )^{M}_{E_a}\; (^{2B}_{E_a - E_n}\; )^{2T}_{E_a + E_n}\; (^{M}_{E_a}
$$
and the **singleton factor** $\mathcal{C}_{\mathrm{sing}}$ has
$$
\mathrm{br}(\mathcal{C}_{\mathrm{sing}})(S) \;=\; )^{S}_{E_n}.
$$
The bracketing of $\pi$ is the concatenation of factor brackets in convex
chain order, followed by the singleton block; cancellation is Kashiwara's `(`
followed by `)` propagating across factor boundaries; $e_n$ acts on the
rightmost surviving `)` and applies its label-determined step type.

The **doubled interior brackets** $(^{2B} \, )^{2T}$ together with the
mid-framing $)^M \ldots (^M$ are the bracket-level expression of the
short-long Cartan entry $a_{n-1,n} = -2$. This is the new building block
that extends Watanabe (arXiv:2110.07177, Props 5.1.1 + 5.1.3) past the
simply-laced restriction (S1)–(S3)'.

(C1) **Per-node compatibility.** $\mathcal{C}_{\mathrm{sing}} \cong
\mathbb{K}[B_n]$ as an ι-crystal, matching Watanabe §4.1 split-node case
$U^i_i = \mathbb{K}[B_i]$ with $\mathrm{sgn}(s_i)$-action. ✓

(C3) **Catalog match at $B_2$.** All 6 net moves of $e_n^2$ on the slice
$S_n = \{\varepsilon_n \geq 2\}$ are realised by this rule. ✓

(C4) **Type-uniform.** The rule is stated uniformly across $B_n$ for
$n \geq 2$. ✓

(C2) iSerre compatibility deferred to Sub-problem 4.

## 1. Setup

$\mathfrak{g}$ of type $B_n$, $n \geq 2$, simple roots
$\alpha_1, \ldots, \alpha_n$ with $\alpha_n = E_n$ short and
$\alpha_a = E_a - E_{a+1}$ long for $a < n$. The Cartan matrix entry
$a_{n-1,n} = -2$ is the short-long edge violating the Watanabe simply-laced
condition (S1)–(S3)'.

Positive roots of $B_n$ partition into $\alpha_n$-strings:
- For each $a \in \{1, \ldots, n-1\}$: the length-3 chain
  $\mathrm{Chain}(a) = \{E_a - E_n,\, E_a,\, E_a + E_n\}$ (bot, mid, top).
- The length-1 singleton $\mathrm{Sing} = \{E_n\}$.
- Non-touching positive roots (those with $\langle \beta, \alpha_n^\vee\rangle = 0$
  and $\beta \pm \alpha_n \notin \Phi^+$, e.g.\ $E_a \pm E_b$ for
  $a \neq b < n$): trivial for the $B_n$ action; ignored below since they
  contribute nothing to the bracketing.

The CST bracketing $S_n(\pi)$ (per b_i_b{2,3,4}.py and the §1 of
2026-05-15-three-strand-braid-Bn.md):
$$
S_n(\pi) \;=\; \bigl[\,)^{M_a}_{E_a}\; (^{2B_a}_{E_a-E_n}\; )^{2T_a}_{E_a+E_n}\; (^{M_a}_{E_a}\,\bigr]_{a=1}^{n-1}\; )^{S}_{E_n}
$$
where $M_a, B_a, T_a$ are the chain-$a$ mid/bot/top counts and $S$ is the
singleton count. Convex order over chains: chain 1 first, then chain 2, etc.

Cancellation: repeatedly delete any adjacent `(` immediately followed by `)`,
across factor boundaries. The canonical $S_n^c(\pi)$ has all surviving `)`s
left of all surviving `(`s. $\varepsilon_n(\pi) =$ # surviving `)`,
$\varphi_n(\pi) =$ # surviving `(`. The action $e_n$ replaces the rightmost
surviving `)` (with label $\beta$) by $e_{\beta - \alpha_n}$ (or removes if
$\beta = \alpha_n$).

## 2. The chain crystal $\mathcal{C}_a$

**Definition 2.1.** The *chain crystal of type B short-long edge* is the
ι-crystal
$$
\mathcal{C}_a \;:=\; \mathbb{K}\bigl\langle (M, B, T) : M, B, T \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}\bigr\rangle,
$$
i.e., the $\mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}^3$-graded vector space generated by chain-mid
($E_a$), chain-bot ($E_a - E_n$), chain-top ($E_a + E_n$).

Its internal bracketing is
$$
\mathrm{br}(\mathcal{C}_a)(M, B, T) \;=\; )^{M}_{E_a}\; (^{2B}_{E_a-E_n}\; )^{2T}_{E_a+E_n}\; (^{M}_{E_a}.
$$

Per-factor cancellation pairs $(^{2B}_{E_a-E_n}$ with $)^{2T}_{E_a+E_n}$
internally:
$$
\mathrm{br}^c(\mathcal{C}_a)(M, B, T) =
\begin{cases}
)^{M}_{E_a}\; )^{2(T-B)}_{E_a+E_n}\; (^{M}_{E_a} & T \geq B, \\[2pt]
)^{M}_{E_a}\; (^{2(B-T)}_{E_a-E_n}\; (^{M}_{E_a} & T < B.
\end{cases}
$$

The per-factor $\varepsilon_n^{\mathcal{C}_a}(M,B,T) = M + 2\max(0, T - B)$ and
$\varphi_n^{\mathcal{C}_a}(M,B,T) = M + 2\max(0, B - T)$. The per-factor
action of $e_n$ (when isolated) is:
- If $T > B$: step type $\mathrm{TM}(a)$, $(M, B, T) \to (M+1, B, T-1)$.
- Else if $M > 0$: step type $\mathrm{MB}(a)$, $(M, B, T) \to (M-1, B+1, T)$.
- Else: zero.

## 3. The singleton crystal $\mathcal{C}_{\mathrm{sing}}$

**Definition 3.1.** The *singleton ι-crystal at the short simple* is
$$
\mathcal{C}_{\mathrm{sing}} \;:=\; \mathbb{K}[B_n] \;\cong\; \bigoplus_{S \geq 0} \mathbb{K}\,B_n^S,
$$
i.e., the polynomial ring on the short simple itself.

Internal bracketing: $\mathrm{br}(\mathcal{C}_{\mathrm{sing}})(S) = )^{S}_{E_n}$.

Per-factor $\varepsilon_n = S$, $\varphi_n = 0$; per-factor
$e_n: S \mapsto S - 1$ (Sing step type), $f_n: S \mapsto S + 1$.

**Lemma 3.2 (Watanabe per-node match).** $\mathcal{C}_{\mathrm{sing}}$ as an
ι-crystal coincides with Watanabe (arXiv:2110.07177) §4.1 split-node rank-1
icrystal $U^i_i = \mathbb{K}[B_i]$ at $a_{i, \tau(i)} = 2$, with the
$\mathrm{sgn}(s_i)$-action $B_n: B_n^S \mapsto B_n^{S+1}$.

*Proof.* Direct from definitions. The crystal generator is the polynomial
generator $B_n$; the lowering operator $e_n$ is "decrement the exponent" and
the raising operator $f_n$ is "increment the exponent"; the icrystal
generator $B_n = e_n + f_n$ at $q = 0$ acts as $S \mapsto S - 1$ plus
$S \mapsto S + 1$. This is exactly Watanabe's split-node rule. ∎

## 4. The tensor product rule

**Definition 4.1 (tensor product of factor crystals).** Given ι-crystal
factors $\mathcal{C}_a$ ($a \in \{1, \ldots, n-1\}$) and
$\mathcal{C}_{\mathrm{sing}}$, the tensor product
$\mathcal{C}_1 \otimes \cdots \otimes \mathcal{C}_{n-1} \otimes \mathcal{C}_{\mathrm{sing}}$
is the $\mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}^{3(n-1) + 1}$-graded vector space with bracketing
$$
\mathrm{br}\bigl((M_a, B_a, T_a)_a, S\bigr)
\;=\;
\mathrm{br}(\mathcal{C}_1)(M_1, B_1, T_1)\;\cdot\;\mathrm{br}(\mathcal{C}_2)(M_2, B_2, T_2)\;\cdots\;\mathrm{br}(\mathcal{C}_{\mathrm{sing}})(S)
$$
(concatenation in convex chain order, then singleton). Cancellation is
applied to the entire concatenated bracket: repeatedly delete adjacent `(`
followed by `)`, across factor boundaries.

The ι-crystal operators on the tensor product:
- $\varepsilon_n(\pi) =$ # surviving `)` in $\mathrm{br}^c(\pi)$.
- $\varphi_n(\pi) =$ # surviving `(`.
- $e_n(\pi)$: act on the rightmost surviving `)` of $\mathrm{br}^c(\pi)$,
  with label $\beta$; the action on $\pi$ is $-e_\beta + e_{\beta - \alpha_n}$
  (or just $-e_\beta$ if $\beta = \alpha_n$).
- $f_n(\pi)$: act on the leftmost surviving `(`, with label $\gamma$; the
  action is $-e_\gamma + e_{\gamma + \alpha_n}$ (or $+e_{\alpha_n}$ if no
  surviving `(` exists).
- $B_n(\pi) = e_n(\pi) + f_n(\pi)$ at $q = 0$.

**Theorem 4.2 (Identification with $\mathrm{Kp}(\infty)$).** As a graded
vector space and as an ι-crystal under $B_n$-action,
$$
\bigotimes_{a=1}^{n-1} \mathcal{C}_a \;\otimes\; \mathcal{C}_{\mathrm{sing}}
\;\cong\;
\mathrm{Kp}(\infty)\big|_{B_n\text{-action}},
$$
via $(M_a, B_a, T_a)_a \otimes S \;\mapsto\; \sum_a \bigl(M_a E_a + B_a (E_a - E_n) + T_a (E_a + E_n)\bigr) + S E_n$.

*Proof.* The bracketing rule on the tensor product (Definition 4.1) is
*literally* the CST bracketing $S_n(\pi)$ via the formula in §1 — both are
concatenations of chain-$a$ blocks in convex order followed by the singleton
block. The action of $e_n, f_n$ via rightmost/leftmost surviving brackets is
CST Def 2.14. Hence the two structures coincide. ∎

**Remark 4.3 (Non-touching roots).** Roots $\beta \in \Phi^+$ with
$\beta \pm \alpha_n \notin \Phi^+ \cup \{0\}$ and
$\langle \beta, \alpha_n^\vee\rangle = 0$ (i.e., $\beta = E_a - E_b$ or
$E_a + E_b$ with $a, b < n$) form *extra factors* in the tensor decomposition
of the full $\mathrm{Kp}(\infty)$, but each is a 1-dim ι-crystal under
$B_n$-action (trivial: $e_n = f_n = 0$, $\varepsilon_n = \varphi_n = 0$).
They commute with everything and contribute nothing to the bracketing. We
omit them above for brevity; including them adds
$(n-1)(n-2) / 2 + (n-1)(n-2)/2 = (n-1)(n-2)$ trivial factors, irrelevant to
the $B_n$ action.

## 5. (C3) Catalog match at $B_2$

At $B_2$: one chain ($a = 1$) and one singleton. The 3 step types are
$\mathrm{MB}(1)$, $\mathrm{TM}(1)$, $\mathrm{Sing}$, with deltas:
$\delta_{\mathrm{MB}(1)} = -e_{E_1} + e_{E_1 - E_2}$,
$\delta_{\mathrm{TM}(1)} = -e_{E_1 + E_2} + e_{E_1}$,
$\delta_{\mathrm{Sing}} = -e_{E_2}$.

The 6 catalog moves are indexed by unordered pairs (with repetition) from
$\{\mathrm{MB}(1), \mathrm{TM}(1), \mathrm{Sing}\}$:

| Pair $\{p, q\}$ | $\delta_p + \delta_q$ | Witness $\pi$ |
|---|---|---|
| $\{\mathrm{Sing}, \mathrm{Sing}\}$ | $-2 e_{E_2}$ | $2 E_2$ |
| $\{\mathrm{Sing}, \mathrm{MB}(1)\}$ | $-e_{E_2} - e_{E_1} + e_{E_1 - E_2}$ | $E_1 + 2 E_2$ |
| $\{\mathrm{Sing}, \mathrm{TM}(1)\}$ | $-e_{E_2} - e_{E_1 + E_2} + e_{E_1}$ | $(E_1 + E_2) + E_2$ |
| $\{\mathrm{MB}(1), \mathrm{MB}(1)\}$ | $-2 e_{E_1} + 2 e_{E_1 - E_2}$ | $2 E_1$ |
| $\{\mathrm{TM}(1), \mathrm{TM}(1)\}$ | $-2 e_{E_1 + E_2} + 2 e_{E_1}$ | $2(E_1 + E_2)$ |
| $\{\mathrm{MB}(1), \mathrm{TM}(1)\}$ | $-e_{E_1 + E_2} + e_{E_1 - E_2}$ | $E_1 + (E_1 + E_2)$ |

**Theorem 5.1 (Catalog at $B_2$).** For each row of the table above, the
tensor product rule applied to the witness $\pi$ produces $e_n^2(\pi) = \pi + \delta_p + \delta_q$.

*Proof.* Direct computation via the bracketing rule. We verify two
representative rows:

*Row $\{\mathrm{TM}(1), \mathrm{TM}(1)\}$.* $\pi = 2(E_1 + E_2)$.
$(M, B, T) = (0, 0, 2)$ on chain 1, $S = 0$. Bracket: $)^4_{E_1+E_2}$.
$\varepsilon_n = 4$, on slice.
- Step 1: rightmost `)` is top — TM(1), $\pi' = (E_1 + E_2) + E_1$,
  $(M', B', T') = (1, 0, 1)$.
- Bracket $\pi'$: $)_{E_1} )^2_{E_1+E_2} (_{E_1}$ (no cancellation, the
  trailing `(` has nothing to its right). $\varepsilon_n = 3$.
- Step 2: rightmost `)` is again top — TM(1), $\pi'' = 2 E_1$.

Net delta: $-2 e_{E_1+E_2} + 2 e_{E_1}$. ✓

*Row $\{\mathrm{Sing}, \mathrm{MB}(1)\}$.* $\pi = E_1 + 2 E_2$.
$(M, B, T) = (1, 0, 0)$ on chain 1, $S = 2$. Bracket:
$)_{E_1} (_{E_1} )^2_{E_2}$. After cancellation of `(`, `)` at positions 1, 2:
$S_n^c = )_{E_1} )_{E_2}$. $\varepsilon_n = 2$, on slice.
- Step 1: rightmost `)` is singleton — Sing, $\pi' = E_1 + E_2$,
  $(M', B', T') = (1, 0, 0)$, $S' = 1$.
- Bracket $\pi'$: $)_{E_1} (_{E_1} )_{E_2}$. Cancel positions 1, 2:
  $S_n^c = )_{E_1}$. $\varepsilon_n = 1$.
- Step 2: rightmost `)` is mid — MB(1), $\pi'' = (E_1 - E_2) + E_2$.

Net delta: $-e_{E_1} + e_{E_1-E_2} - e_{E_2}$. ✓

The remaining four rows follow by analogous direct computation. (The
verification has been done at b_i_b2.py for the bracketing; since the tensor
product rule is identical to the bracketing by Theorem 4.2, the catalog
match is automatic.) ∎

**Class decomposition at $B_2$:** intra-chain $3(n-1) = 3$ (MM, TT, MT) +
cross-chain $2(n-1)(n-2) = 0$ + singleton-involving $2n - 1 = 3$ (SS, S-MB(1),
S-TM(1)) = 6 = $\binom{2 \cdot 2}{2}$. ✓ (Matches the type-uniform formula
of 2026-05-15-three-strand-braid-Bn.md.)

## 6. (C4) Type-uniformity: lift to $B_n$ for $n \geq 2$

The chain factor $\mathcal{C}_a$ and the singleton factor $\mathcal{C}_{\mathrm{sing}}$
are defined uniformly in $n$. At $B_n$ short simple, $\mathrm{Kp}(\infty)|_{B_n}$
is the tensor product of $(n-1)$ chain factors plus 1 singleton factor.

The catalog of $\binom{2n}{2} = n(2n - 1)$ moves is the set of net deltas
$\delta_p + \delta_q$ for unordered pairs from the $2n - 1$ step types:
- $\mathrm{MB}(a)$ for $a = 1, \ldots, n - 1$ (chain-$a$ mid step).
- $\mathrm{TM}(a)$ for $a = 1, \ldots, n - 1$ (chain-$a$ top step).
- $\mathrm{Sing}$ (singleton step).

The class decomposition $3(n-1) + 2(n-1)(n-2) + (2n - 1)$ corresponds to:
- *Intra-chain* (3 per chain × $n-1$ chains): pairs $\{\mathrm{MB}(a), \mathrm{MB}(a)\}$, $\{\mathrm{TM}(a), \mathrm{TM}(a)\}$, $\{\mathrm{MB}(a), \mathrm{TM}(a)\}$.
- *Cross-chain* (4 per chain pair × $\binom{n-1}{2}$ pairs): pairs $\{\mathrm{MB}(a) \text{ or } \mathrm{TM}(a),\, \mathrm{MB}(b) \text{ or } \mathrm{TM}(b)\}$ for $a < b$.
- *Singleton-involving* ($1 + 2(n-1) = 2n-1$): pairs containing $\mathrm{Sing}$.

The match between the structural counts and the tensor product rule is *by
construction*: each pair $\{p, q\}$ is realised by a witness $\pi$ whose
nonzero multiplicities lie in $p$'s factor + $q$'s factor (intra-chain when
$p, q$ from same chain factor; cross-chain when from different factors;
singleton-involving when at least one is in $\mathcal{C}_{\mathrm{sing}}$).
The realisability of all pairs is the content of three-strand-braid §4
Theorem 4.1 (iii), already proven type-uniformly.

**Hence the tensor product rule reproduces the catalog at $B_n$ for all
$n \geq 2$, in particular matching the empirically verified catalogs at
$B_2$ (6 moves), $B_3$ (15), $B_4$ (28).**

## 8. Comparison with Watanabe

Watanabe arXiv:2110.07177 Props 5.1.1 + 5.1.3 give a tensor product rule for
ι-crystals at simply-laced edges ($a_{ij} \in \{0, -1\}$). In Watanabe's
setup:
- Each node $i$ has a per-node icrystal $U^i_i$ (§4.1, three cases by
  $a_{i, \tau(i)} \in \{2, 0, -1\}$).
- Tensor products propagate the $B_i$-action across factors via Kashiwara
  signature rules conditioned by (S1)–(S3)' on the off-diagonal $a_{ij}$.

Type BDI ($\tau = \mathrm{id}$, all nodes split, $a_{i, \tau(i)} = 2$ for all
$i$): per-node objects $U^i_i = \mathbb{K}[B_i]$ at every node (Watanabe
§4.1). The off-diagonal $a_{ij}$: at the long-long edges $a_{a, b} = -1$
($|a - b| = 1, a, b < n$), Watanabe Prop 5.1.1 applies. At the **short-long
edge** $a_{n-1, n} = -2$, Prop 5.1.1 does *not* apply (violates (S1)–(S3)').

**Rick's tensor product rule fills exactly this gap.** Specifically:
- The singleton factor $\mathcal{C}_{\mathrm{sing}}$ is Watanabe's per-node
  $U^n_n = \mathbb{K}[B_n]$ for the short simple.
- The chain factor $\mathcal{C}_a$ is the *new* building block for the
  short-long-edge tensor product. It encodes the $a_{n-1, n} = -2$
  contribution via doubled interior brackets.
- The tensor product rule across $\mathcal{C}_a$'s and $\mathcal{C}_{\mathrm{sing}}$
  is Kashiwara's signature rule, extended past Watanabe's (S1)–(S3)'
  restriction.

This is the precise statement: **Rick's tensor product rule extends Watanabe
Prop 5.1.1 across the short-long edge.**

## 9. The "non-touching" factors and the long-simple action

For completeness: $\mathrm{Kp}(\infty)$ of full $\mathfrak{g} = B_n$ has
positive roots beyond those in $\alpha_n$-strings; specifically
$E_a \pm E_b$ for $a \neq b$, $a, b < n$. These have
$\langle E_a \pm E_b, \alpha_n^\vee\rangle = 0$ and $E_a \pm E_b \pm \alpha_n
\notin \Phi^+$, so they are *non-touching at $\alpha_n$* — they contribute
nothing to $S_n(\pi)$ and are invariant under $B_n$.

But these non-touching roots **do** participate in the long-simple actions
$B_a$ for $a < n$. The Kashiwara $\alpha_a$-strings for long simple
$\alpha_a = E_a - E_{a+1}$ partition $\Phi^+$ differently — into length-2
chains and singletons. At long simples, the tensor product rule is
*Watanabe-simply-laced* (no chain has doubled brackets); per-node
$U^a_a = \mathbb{K}[B_a]$ (split node, $a_{a, \tau(a)} = 2$); cross-edges
between long simples are $a_{ab} = -1$ (Watanabe Prop 5.1.1 applies); only
the cross-edge $a_{n-1, n} = -2$ is new.

So Rick's tensor product rule applies *at the short simple only*; at long
simples, Watanabe Prop 5.1.1 already suffices. The full type-BDI ι-crystal
is the **gluing** of:
- Watanabe Prop 5.1.1 at each $a_{ab} = -1$ edge (long-long).
- Rick's chain-factor rule at the $a_{n-1, n} = -2$ edge (short-long).
- Watanabe §4.1 per-node $\mathbb{K}[B_i]$ at each split node.

## 10.5. Cross-chain emergence (Sub-problem 2 partial, at $B_3$)

The 4 cross-chain catalog entries at $B_3$ correspond to unordered pairs
$\{\sigma_1, \sigma_2\}$ with $\sigma_1 \in \{\mathrm{MB}(1), \mathrm{TM}(1)\}$
(chain A) and $\sigma_2 \in \{\mathrm{MB}(2), \mathrm{TM}(2)\}$ (chain B):

| Entry | Pair | Witness $\pi$ | Tensor factor: $(M_1, B_1, T_1) \otimes (M_2, B_2, T_2) \otimes S$ |
|---|---|---|---|
| $AB_{TT}$ | $\{\mathrm{TM}(1), \mathrm{TM}(2)\}$ | $(E_1 + E_3) + E_1 + (E_2 + E_3)$ | $(1, 0, 1) \otimes (0, 0, 1) \otimes 0$ |
| $AB_{TM}$ | $\{\mathrm{TM}(1), \mathrm{MB}(2)\}$ | $(E_1 + E_3) + E_2$ | $(0, 0, 1) \otimes (1, 0, 0) \otimes 0$ |
| $AB_{MT}$ | $\{\mathrm{MB}(1), \mathrm{TM}(2)\}$ | $E_1 + (E_2 + E_3)$ | $(1, 0, 0) \otimes (0, 0, 1) \otimes 0$ |
| $AB_{MM}$ | $\{\mathrm{MB}(1), \mathrm{MB}(2)\}$ | $E_1 + 2 E_2$ | $(1, 0, 0) \otimes (2, 0, 0) \otimes 0$ |

**Mechanism.** In each cross-chain witness, the trailing $(^{M_1}_{E_1}$ of
chain A's bracket block cross-cancels with the leading $)$s of chain B
(either its mid-`)`s for MB(2) or its top-`)`s for TM(2), depending on
chain B's bracket configuration). After cross-cancellation, the rightmost
surviving `)` is a chain-B symbol, so step 1 acts on chain B. After step 1,
the bracket reorganises and the rightmost surviving `)` becomes a chain-A
symbol, so step 2 acts on chain A.

**Observation (step order).** In all 4 cross-chain entries at $B_3$, the
first step acts on the *later* chain (chain B, convex-order index 2), and
the second step on the *earlier* chain (chain A, convex-order index 1).
This is the bracket-level manifestation of the "trailing-(s eat leading-)s"
cross-factor cancellation: the later chain's leading-)s are reduced by the
earlier chain's trailing-(s, exposing the later chain's residual-)s as
rightmost. Hence the first step lands on the later chain.

**General statement (Sub-problem 2 lift to $B_n$).** For each chain pair
$1 \leq a < b \leq n-1$ and each ordered pair
$(\sigma_b, \sigma_a) \in \{\mathrm{MB}(b), \mathrm{TM}(b)\} \times \{\mathrm{MB}(a), \mathrm{TM}(a)\}$,
there is a witness $\pi_{ab}^{\sigma_b \sigma_a}$ on which $e_n^2$ acts as the
two-step sequence $\sigma_b$ then $\sigma_a$, with net delta
$\delta_{\sigma_b} + \delta_{\sigma_a}$. There are
$4 \binom{n-1}{2} = 2(n-1)(n-2)$ such witnesses, matching the cross-chain
class count of three-strand-braid Theorem 5.1. The witnesses generalise the
$B_3$ table: the structure is $\pi_a^{\sigma_a\text{-bridge}} \otimes \pi_b^{\sigma_b\text{-target}}$,
