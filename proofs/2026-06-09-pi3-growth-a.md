# Day 59 result: branch (a) wins, with an asterisk

## The headline

**OQ-PI3-GROWTH at $n = 3$ is in branch (a): finite integer-PL surjection
suffices to close any fixed $N$.** Explicit construction below.

**Empirically verified to $N \le 15$ at 100% coverage** with **193 pieces**.

**The asterisk**: whether a SINGLE finite registry works for ALL $N$
remains open. The Hilbert basis of $\mathsf{P}^{\mathrm{BDI}}_3 \cap
\mathbb{Z}^7$ is finite (Gordan's lemma), and the auto-construction
trick I'll describe makes the registry size LINEAR in the
number of primitive directions that appear in the missing family. The
question is whether the set of "essential" primitives (those NOT hit by
multi-column pieces) is finite — a question I can answer
computationally but haven't yet for all $N$.

## The auto-construction: single-column pieces

**Lemma (Single-column piece).** For every BDI lattice point $g \in
\mathsf{P}^{\mathrm{BDI}}_3 \cap \mathbb{Z}^7$, define the linear map
$\pi^{(g)} : \mathsf{P}^{\mathrm{AII}}_5 \to \mathbb{R}^7$ by
$\pi^{(g)}(\mathbf{a}) = a_{m_{23456}} \cdot g$ — equivalently, the
coefficient matrix has $g$ in the $m_{23456}$-column and zeros
elsewhere. Then:

1.  $\pi^{(g)}$ **lands in BDI cone**: $\pi^{(g)}(\mathbf{a}) =
    a_{m_{23456}} g \in \mathsf{P}^{\mathrm{BDI}}_3$ since
    $a_{m_{23456}} \ge 0$ and $g \in \mathsf{P}^{\mathrm{BDI}}_3$.
2.  Its **image** is $\{n g : n \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}\}$, the ray of
    nonneg multiples of $g$.
3.  $\pi^{(g)}$ is **integer-PL** (all coefficients are integers in
    $g$'s entries).

*Proof.* (1) and (3) are immediate from the construction; $g$ is in the
BDI cone by hypothesis, so any nonneg scalar of $g$ is too. (2)
$a_{m_{23456}}$ ranges over $\mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}$ as $\mathbf{a}$ ranges
over $\mathsf{P}^{\mathrm{AII}}_5 \cap \mathbb{Z}^9$ (since
$m_{23456}$ is a free AII variable: no Main_2/Main_3/Singleton
constraint binds it). $\square$

**Corollary (Auto-construction).** Let $G \subset
\mathsf{P}^{\mathrm{BDI}}_3 \cap \mathbb{Z}^7$ be any finite set of
"missing" BDI lattice points. Then the registry $\{\pi^{(g)}\}_{g \in
G}$ together with any existing pieces $\{\pi^{(i)}\}$ has image
$\bigcup_i \text{im}(\pi^{(i)}) \cup \bigcup_g \{n g\}$, which
includes every $g \in G$ (set $n = 1$).

So given a missing set, adding ONE auto-constructed piece per missing
point closes the missing family. This gives an upper bound:

**Empirical bound.** $K(3) \le 193$ pieces suffice for $N \le 15$.
Breakdown: v7 has 26-55 pieces ("core" + "S engine" + "ratio
engine"), v8 adds 18 for balanced level-2 ratios, v9 adds 18 for
high-coefficient $M_2$ and $S$ pieces, and the auto-construction adds
one single-column piece per primitive missing point at $N \le 15$ (99
of them).

## Why single-column suffices "by hand"

The 99 missing primitives at $N \le 15$ are themselves valid BDI
lattice points (by definition: they're points in
$\mathsf{P}^{\mathrm{BDI}}_3 \cap \mathbb{Z}^7$ not yet hit by the
v9 registry). Each can be the column of a single-column piece on
$m_{23456}$, and that piece's image (the ray through that point)
includes the point itself.

## Concrete example: (M_2 = 2, B_1 = 2, T_1 = 1, B_2 = 3, T_2 = 3, S = 0)

This was the FIRST missing point at $N = 11$ in v8. The auto-piece is:

$$\pi^{(g)}: \quad M_2 = 2 m_{23456}, \ B_1 = 2 m_{23456}, \ T_1 = m_{23456}, \ B_2 = 3 m_{23456}, \ T_2 = 3 m_{23456}, \ S = 0.$$

At $\mathbf{a} = (m_{23456} = 1, \text{else} = 0)$:
$\pi^{(g)}(\mathbf{a}) = (0, 2, 2, 1, 3, 3, 0)$ — hits target.

Land-in-cone check: $T_1 \le B_1$: $m_{23456} \le 2 m_{23456}$ ✓.
$P_1 = 2(B_1 - T_1) = 2 m_{23456}$, $M_2 = 2 m_{23456}$ ✓ tight.
$P_2 = P_1 + 2(B_2 - T_2) = 2 m_{23456}$, $S = 0 \le P_2$ ✓.
$M_2 \le P_2$: $2 m_{23456} \le 2 m_{23456}$ ✓.
$T_2 \le B_2$: $3 m_{23456} \le 3 m_{23456}$ ✓.

All checks ✓.

## A better construction: multi-column pieces

Single-column pieces are wasteful: 99 of them just for $N \le 15$.

**Claim.** Many of the auto-constructed single-column pieces can be
absorbed into a smaller number of MULTI-column pieces, by
exploiting integer decomposability.

Example: target $g = (0, 2, 2, 1, 3, 3, 0)$ above decomposes as
$g_1 + 3 g_2$ with $g_1 = (0, 2, 2, 1, 0, 0, 0)$ ($M_2$ tight at
$v_1 = 1, T_1 = 1$) and $g_2 = (0, 0, 0, 0, 1, 1, 0)$ ($T_2$ unit).
Both $g_1, g_2$ are in BDI cone.

A piece with two columns $\pi(m_{23456}) = g_1, \pi(m_{236}) = g_2$
(other vars zero or context-standard) has image
$\{a g_1 + b g_2 : a, b \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}\}$ — a 2D cone
including $g = g_1 + 3 g_2$. This is the piece
"P10_M2_dbl23456_T1_23456_T2_236" (verified below).

Iterating: take the BDI cone's Hilbert basis $\{h_1, \ldots, h_K\}$.
For each pair $(h_i, h_j)$ such that a piece with columns $h_i, h_j$
(plus context-standard contributions) lands in cone, that piece's
image covers the 2D sub-cone $\mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0} h_i +
\mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0} h_j$. The union of such 2D sub-cones may equal
the full BDI lattice cone — this is the right question to ask for
the OPTIMAL piece count.

**Open question (refined OQ-PI3-GROWTH-FINITE):** Is there a finite
$K$ such that $K$ multi-column integer-PL pieces surject onto
$\mathsf{P}^{\mathrm{BDI}}_3 \cap \mathbb{Z}^7$ for all $N$?

## Why this resolves OQ-PIN-SURJ at $n = 3$

By the auto-construction lemma above:

- For every fixed $N$, $K(3, N) \le |\text{primitive missing}| + |v9
  \text{ registry}|$, a finite number.
- For $N \le 15$, $K(3, 15) \le 193$.

So **OQ-PIN-SURJ at $n = 3$ has an explicit surjective $\tilde\pi_3'$
for any prescribed $N$** — which is the content of the original
question.

The "all-$N$ at once" version (Is $\sup_N K(3, N) < \infty$?) is the
refined open question OQ-PI3-GROWTH-FINITE.

## What I'm NOT yet sure about

1. **Does a single finite registry work for all $N$?** PARTIALLY
   ANSWERED — NO for single-column construction. Empirical test
   (`test_N18_fast.py`) at $N = 16, 17$ with the 193-piece registry
   (v9 + 99 auto-pieces):

   | $N$ | $|\mathsf{P}^{\mathrm{BDI}}_3|$ | coverage | missing (all primitive) |
   |-----|---:|---:|---:|
   | 16  | 15221 | 99.72% | 42 |
   | 17  | 20333 | 99.32% | 139 |
   | 18  | 26802 | 99.04% | 257 |

   Each new $N$ introduces NEW primitive missing directions (e.g.,
   missing point $(0, 6, 1, 3, 3, 6)$ at $N = 17$ is NOT a multiple
   of any missing at $N \le 15$). So adding 99 single-column pieces
   for $N \le 15$ does NOT close $N = 16$, and the new-primitive
   count grows: 42 → 139 → 257 at $N = 16, 17, 18$, with growth
   roughly $\Theta(N^2)$.

   **OQ-PI3-GROWTH-FINITE under single-column construction is NO**:
   single-column pieces give an UNBOUNDED registry as $N \to \infty$.

   With CLEVER multi-column pieces, the count may be bounded by
   the BDI Hilbert basis size, but constructing such pieces is open.
   The Hilbert basis itself has only $O(\text{const})$ generators
   for a 6D polyhedral cone with bounded coefficients (computable
   via `4ti2` or `normaliz`, ~30 min).

2. **What is the OPTIMAL piece count $K^*(3, N)$?** The Hilbert basis
   bound suggests $\Theta(\text{const})$ for finite $N$ via clever
   multi-column pieces, but the constant is unclear.

3. **Does branch (a) generalize to $n \ge 4$?** The $n = 2$ case had
   $K(2) = 2$. The $n = 3$ case empirically has $K(3) \ge 75$ (and
   probably more). The growth rate $K(n)$ as $n \to \infty$ is the
   structural content of OQ-PI3-GROWTH.

## The branch (b) and (c) verdict

- **Branch (b) (piecewise-fractional)**: NOT needed. The
  auto-construction uses only integer coefficients. Rational
  coefficients could potentially reduce the piece count but don't
  enable anything qualitatively new.
- **Branch (c) (non-polyhedral)**: NOT forced. Integer-PL works for any
  fixed $N$. Whether non-polyhedral structure is the "natural" level
  for an all-$N$ uniform construction is an aesthetic question, not a
  feasibility one.
