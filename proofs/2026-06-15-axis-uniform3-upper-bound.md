# §1. Recap of Gap D and what Day 70 delivers

Day 69 (`proofs/2026-06-14-axis-uniform3-proof.md`) shipped the
**lower bound** $\#\mathrm{AXIS}(n) \ge 3$ uniformly in $n \ge 3$ via
three structural 3-piece families (R-double / free-top prefix /
free-bottom long). The **upper bound** Lemma D was articulated as
open, with three sub-items:

1. Precise definition of "minimal cover."
2. Characterisation of pieces hitting "novel" BDI lattice points.
3. Count-≤-2 combinatorial argument for non-canonical routings of each
   $c \notin \{p_1, p_n, l_1\}$.

**Day 70 contribution.**

- **(1) RESOLVED** (§3). Target $T_n = P^{\mathrm{BDI}}_\mathbb{Z}$.
  A minimal cover is a smallest finite set of feasible pieces whose
  union of images contains $T_n$.

- **(2) RESOLVED** (§4-5). The **Feasibility Ray-Characterisation**
  (Theorem 4.2) is the workhorse: a piece's feasibility is equivalent
  to a finite list of $3n$ column-level conditions corresponding to
  the AII cone rays. Corollary 5.1 expresses $\mathrm{Im}(\pi)$ as the
  $\mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}$-semigroup generated by $\pi$ applied to those
  rays.

- **(3) REFRAMED AND PARTIALLY PROVED** (§6-7). Instead of "≤ 2
  distinct routings" (which depends on cover choice), we attack the
  cleaner **3-clique non-existence**:

  **Conjecture D-3clique:** For every $n \ge 3$ and every coord
  $c \notin \{p_1, p_n, l_1\}$, no three feasible pieces $\pi_0, \pi_1, \pi_2$
  exist that pairwise differ only on column $c$ with three distinct
  $c$-columns.

  This directly implies the strict # AXIS upper bound (no 3-clique ⇒
  fewer than 3 pair-collisions in pieces sharing all-but-one column,
  combined with structural sparsity of "twin pieces" empirically
  observed: at most 1 piece per distinct (other-column) profile).

  Day 70 proves Conjecture D-3clique for:
  - **c = l_n** ($l_n$ at all $n$): RIGID by image-redundancy.
  - **c = p_{n-1}** ($p_{n-1}$ middle): RIGID by image-redundancy.
  - **c = l_j$ ($2 \le j \le n - 1$):** at most 2 image-classes
    (canonical $\to M_j$, divert $\to S$), hence BINARY at most.
  - **c = s_j** ($j \ge 1$): at most 2 image-classes (balanced
    $(B_j, T_j)$, divert $\to S$), hence BINARY at most.
  - **c = $\Lambda$** (at even $n$): RIGID by analogous argument.

  The remaining open case is the **interior prefix** $c = p_i$ for
  $1 < i < n - 1$, reduced to a clean **Conjecture D-pi**.

# §2. Setup (reprise)

We use the Day-69 §2 setup. Briefly:

- AII coordinates: $p_1, \ldots, p_n, l_1, \ldots, l_n, s_1, \ldots, s_n$
  (at odd $n$). At even $n$, replace $s_n$ with $\Lambda = \sum_{i=1}^{n-1} s_i$.

- AII polytope $P^{\mathrm{AII}}_n$: non-negativity + Main_i
  ($l_i + s_i \le p_{i-1}$, $i = 2, \ldots, n$). At $n = 3$
  additionally the Singleton constraint.

- BDI polytope $P^{\mathrm{BDI}}_n$: coords
  $M_2, \ldots, M_{n-1}, B_1, T_1, \ldots, B_{n-1}, T_{n-1}, S$ with
  $T_a \le B_a$, $M_a \le P_{a-1}$, $S \le P_{n-1}$,
  $P_a = 2 \sum_{b \le a}(B_b - T_b)$, all $\ge 0$.

- A **piece** $\pi$ is an integer matrix in $\mathbb{Z}^{n_{\mathrm{BDI}} \times n_{\mathrm{AII}}}$;
  $\pi^c$ denotes its column on AII coord $c$.

- **Feasibility**: $\pi(p) \in P^{\mathrm{BDI}}_\mathbb{Z}$ for every
  $p \in P^{\mathrm{AII}}_\mathbb{Z}$.

- **Image** $\mathrm{Im}(\pi) := \pi(P^{\mathrm{AII}}_\mathbb{Z}) \subseteq P^{\mathrm{BDI}}_\mathbb{Z}$.

# §3. The target set $T_n$ and minimal covers

**Definition 3.1.** $T_n := P^{\mathrm{BDI}}_\mathbb{Z}$, i.e., all
BDI integer points satisfying the BDI defining inequalities.

**Definition 3.2.** A **cover** is a finite set of feasible pieces
$\mathcal{C} = \{\pi_1, \ldots, \pi_k\}$ with
$\bigcup_i \mathrm{Im}(\pi_i) \supseteq T_n$.

**Definition 3.3.** A cover $\mathcal{C}$ is **minimal** if removing
any piece $\pi_i$ breaks the cover property:
$\mathrm{Im}(\pi_i) \not\subseteq \bigcup_{j \ne i} \mathrm{Im}(\pi_j)$.

**Empirical verification.** Day-58 verified $\mathcal{C}_3^{55}$
(55-piece cover at $n = 3$) achieves 100% coverage at $N \le 10$.
Day-68 verified $\mathcal{C}_3^{26}$ (26-piece minimal cover, the
"`MIN_COVER_26`") achieves 100% coverage at $N \le 5$ and likely
beyond (verified today: 100% at $N = 5$, 130/130 BDI pts).

At $n = 5$, the Day-68 27-piece registry covers a substantial fraction
of $T_5$ but has not been verified to be a minimal cover of all $T_5$ —
it's the analog of the design cover at $n = 5$, sufficient for the
AXIS analysis but not necessarily complete.

For the upper bound argument, we focus on the **design cover**: the
canonical construction (base piece + R-double family + Lemma B/C
variants + bulk-completion pieces).

# §4. Feasibility Ray-Characterisation (NEW)

This is the key new tool. It replaces the global condition
"piece feasible on all AII lattice points" with finite column-level
checks.

## 4.1. AII cone rays

**Lemma 4.1 (AII cone rays).** The AII cone at odd $n \ge 3$ (no
Singleton, so $n \ge 5$) has exactly $3n$ extreme rays:

- $\mathcal{R}_{p_j} = e_{p_j}$ for $j = 1, \ldots, n$ ($n$ rays).
- $\mathcal{R}_{l_1} = e_{l_1}$, $\mathcal{R}_{s_1} = e_{s_1}$ (2 rays).
- $\mathcal{R}_{l_j} = e_{p_{j-1}} + e_{l_j}$ for $j = 2, \ldots, n$
  ($n - 1$ rays).
- $\mathcal{R}_{s_j} = e_{p_{j-1}} + e_{s_j}$ for $j = 2, \ldots, n$
  ($n - 1$ rays).

Total: $n + 2 + (n - 1) + (n - 1) = 3n$ rays.

*Proof.* The AII cone is the polyhedral cone in $\mathbb{R}^{3n}$
defined by $3n$ non-negativity inequalities + $n - 1$ Main_i
inequalities ($i = 2, \ldots, n$).

Existence: for any AII point $p$, set $\delta_j = p_{j-1} - l_j - s_j$
(Main_j slack, $\ge 0$). Then
$$
p = \sum_{j=2}^n \delta_j\, \mathcal{R}_{p_{j-1}} + p_n \mathcal{R}_{p_n}
   + l_1 \mathcal{R}_{l_1} + s_1 \mathcal{R}_{s_1}
   + \sum_{j=2}^n l_j \mathcal{R}_{l_j} + \sum_{j=2}^n s_j \mathcal{R}_{s_j}.
$$
All coefficients $\ge 0$, so $p$ is in the conic hull of the rays.
Note: the term $\sum_{j=2}^n \delta_j \mathcal{R}_{p_{j-1}}$ contributes
to $p_1, \ldots, p_{n-1}$ (depending on which $j$).

Extremality: each ray lies on $3n - 1$ independent facets of the cone
(coordinate hyperplanes + appropriate Main facets). Verification:

- $e_{p_j}$: lies on $\{p_b = 0\}$ ($b \ne j$, $n - 1$ facets),
  $\{l_a = 0\}$ ($n$ facets), $\{s_a = 0\}$ ($n$ facets). Total
  $3n - 1$ non-negativity facets, all independent. ✓

- $\mathcal{R}_{l_j} = e_{p_{j-1}} + e_{l_j}$ ($j \ge 2$): lies on
  $\{p_b = 0\}$ ($b \ne j - 1$, $n - 1$ facets), $\{l_a = 0\}$
  ($a \ne j$, $n - 1$ facets), $\{s_a = 0\}$ ($n$ facets), and
  Main_j: $p_{j-1} - l_j - s_j = 0$ (1 facet). Total $3n - 2 + 1 = 3n - 1$.
  Independence: Main_j is independent of the non-negativity facets
  shown. ✓

- $\mathcal{R}_{s_j}$ analogous. ✓

- $e_{l_1}$: $\{p_b = 0\}$ ($n$ facets), $\{l_a = 0\}$ ($a \ne 1$,
  $n - 1$ facets), $\{s_a = 0\}$ ($n$ facets). Total $3n - 1$. ✓

- $e_{s_1}$ analogous. ✓

So the cone has exactly $3n$ extreme rays as listed. $\square$

## 4.2. Feasibility via rays

**Theorem 4.2 (Feasibility Ray-Characterisation).** A piece $\pi$
is feasible iff:

- (F1) $\pi^{p_j} \in P^{\mathrm{BDI}}$ for $j = 1, \ldots, n$.
- (F2) $\pi^{p_{j-1}} + \pi^{l_j} \in P^{\mathrm{BDI}}$ for $j = 2, \ldots, n$.
- (F3) $\pi^{p_{j-1}} + \pi^{s_j} \in P^{\mathrm{BDI}}$ for $j = 2, \ldots, n$.
- (F4) $\pi^{l_1}, \pi^{s_1} \in P^{\mathrm{BDI}}$.

*Proof.* $(\Rightarrow)$: each AII ray is a lattice point in $P^{\mathrm{AII}}_\mathbb{Z}$;
$\pi$ sends it to $P^{\mathrm{BDI}}_\mathbb{Z}$ by feasibility.
The images are $\pi(\mathcal{R}_{p_j}) = \pi^{p_j}$,
$\pi(\mathcal{R}_{l_j}) = \pi^{p_{j-1}} + \pi^{l_j}$, etc.

$(\Leftarrow)$: any $p \in P^{\mathrm{AII}}$ is a non-negative
combination of rays (Lemma 4.1). By linearity of $\pi$ and convexity
(plus integrality) of $P^{\mathrm{BDI}}$, $\pi(p)$ is a non-negative
combination of points in $P^{\mathrm{BDI}}$, hence in
$P^{\mathrm{BDI}}$. $\square$

**Verification.** Sanity-checked against the n=5 27-piece registry
(`code/2026-06-13-n5-axis-count/`): the F1-F4 column-level conditions
match the per-piece feasibility output. (Code verification:
`code/2026-06-15-axis-upper-bound-verify/ray_check.py`.)

## 4.3. Adjustments at $n = 3$ and at even $n$

**$n = 3$ Singleton constraint.** The AII cone at $n = 3$ is
modified by the Singleton constraint $\max(0, l_3 - s_2 - p_2) \le s_1 \le l_3 - s_2$.
This adds extra rays (e.g., $e_{l_3} + e_{s_2}$ as a boundary ray when
$s_1 = l_3 - s_2$). Lemma 4.1 needs refinement at $n = 3$. Day-69
handled the Singleton modification empirically in Lemma A; the
Day-70 Theorem 4.2 applies to $n \ge 5$ directly, and to $n = 3$
modulo Singleton-specific extra constraints (which are handled by the
empirical 26-piece cover verification).

**Even $n$.** The coord $\Lambda$ satisfies the linking equation
$\Lambda = \sum_{i=1}^{n-1} s_i$. Effectively, $\Lambda$ is a derived
coord; the cone has $3n - 1$ extreme rays instead of $3n$, and the
Theorem 4.2 conditions adjust by replacing $\pi^{\Lambda}$-related
conditions with the appropriate derived expression. Specifically,
$\Lambda$ as an independent column with non-negative entries that
project canonically to $(B_{n-1}, T_{n-1})$ balanced.

# §5. Image Semigroup Description

## 5.1. Generators

**Corollary 5.1.** By linearity, for any feasible piece $\pi$,
$$
\mathrm{Im}(\pi) = \left\langle\
\pi^{p_j},\ \pi^{p_{j-1}} + \pi^{l_j},\ \pi^{p_{j-1}} + \pi^{s_j},\
\pi^{l_1},\ \pi^{s_1}
\,\right\rangle_{\mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}}.
$$

This is a $(3n)$-element generating set of an integer cone semigroup
in $\mathbb{Z}^{n_{\mathrm{BDI}}}_{\ge 0}$.

## 5.2. Base piece image

The Day-69 base piece's columns are:
- $\pi^{p_j} = e_{B_j}$ for $j = 1, \ldots, n-1$; $\pi^{p_n} = 0$.
- $\pi^{l_j} = e_{M_j}$ for $j = 2, \ldots, n-1$; $\pi^{l_n} = e_S$;
  $\pi^{l_1} = e_{B_1}$.
- $\pi^{s_j} = e_{B_j} + e_{T_j}$ for $j = 1, \ldots, n-1$;
  $\pi^{s_n} = 0$.

So $\mathrm{Im}(\pi^{\mathrm{base}}_n)$ is generated by:
- $\{e_{B_j}\}_{j=1}^{n-1}$ (from $\pi^{p_j}$ rays for $j \le n - 1$
  and $\pi^{l_1}$).
- $\{e_{B_{j-1}} + e_{M_j}\}_{j=2}^{n-1}$ (from $\pi^{p_{j-1}} + \pi^{l_j}$).
- $e_{B_{n-1}} + e_S$ (from $\pi^{p_{n-1}} + \pi^{l_n}$).
- $\{e_{B_{j-1}} + e_{B_j} + e_{T_j}\}_{j=2}^{n-1}$ (from $\pi^{p_{j-1}} + \pi^{s_j}$).
- $e_{B_1} + e_{T_1}$ (from $\pi^{s_1}$).
- $e_{B_{n-1}}$ (from $\pi^{p_{n-1}} + \pi^{s_n}$, with $\pi^{s_n} = 0$).

## 5.3. Image-containment criterion

**Lemma 5.3.** Two feasible pieces $\pi, \pi'$ satisfy
$\mathrm{Im}(\pi) \subseteq \mathrm{Im}(\pi')$ iff each of the $3n$
generators of $\mathrm{Im}(\pi)$ lies in $\mathrm{Im}(\pi')$.

## 5.4. Column-variant containment

For two pieces $\pi, \pi'$ that differ ONLY on column $c$, the
relevant condition (by Lemma 5.3) reduces to the **$c$-affected
generators** of $\mathrm{Im}(\pi)$:

| $c$ | $c$-affected generators of $\mathrm{Im}(\pi)$ |
|---|---|
| $p_1$ | $\pi^{p_1}$, $\pi^{p_1} + \pi^{l_2}$, $\pi^{p_1} + \pi^{s_2}$ |
| $p_i$ ($1 < i < n$) | $\pi^{p_i}$, $\pi^{p_i} + \pi^{l_{i+1}}$, $\pi^{p_i} + \pi^{s_{i+1}}$ |
| $p_n$ | $\pi^{p_n}$ (only) |
| $l_1$ | $\pi^{l_1}$ (only) |
| $l_j$ ($j \ge 2$) | $\pi^{p_{j-1}} + \pi^{l_j}$ (only) |
| $s_1$ | $\pi^{s_1}$ (only) |
| $s_j$ ($j \ge 2$) | $\pi^{p_{j-1}} + \pi^{s_j}$ (only) |

The AXIS triple $\{p_1, p_n, l_1\}$ contains both:
- $p_1$ (3 affected generators) AND $p_n$, $l_1$ (1 each).

The non-AXIS coords are:
- $p_i$ middle (3 affected generators) AND $l_j$ ($j \ge 2$), $s_j$
  (1 each).

So the # of affected generators does NOT distinguish AXIS from
non-AXIS. The distinction is more subtle: it's about which
$c$-columns can be "realized" by 3 distinct feasible pieces under
the cover-design constraints.

# §6. Image-redundancy lemmas

We use the column-variant containment to prove image-redundancy for
specific routings. The pattern: identify the "canonical" routing and
show any alternative is either image-contained OR contributes a single
distinct image (BINARY), but never produces a 3-clique.

## 6.1. Lemma D-long-n ($c = l_n$): RIGID

**Lemma 6.1.** For every $n \ge 3$, every feasible piece $\pi$ in a
canonical cover has $\pi^{l_n}$ image-equivalent to $e_S$. In
particular, $l_n$ contributes 0 rank-1 piece-pair collisions on
$\{l_n = 0\}$ in the design cover (i.e., $l_n$ is RIGID).

*Proof.* The column $l_n$ affects only one generator:
$\pi^{p_{n-1}} + \pi^{l_n}$.

For the canonical $\pi^{l_n} = e_S$, this generator is
$\pi^{p_{n-1}} + e_S$.

For any other routing $\pi^{l_n} = v$, feasibility (F2 at $j = n$)
requires $\pi^{p_{n-1}} + v \in P^{\mathrm{BDI}}$, and the affected
generator becomes $\pi^{p_{n-1}} + v$.

**Case $v = e_X$ for $X \in \{B_a, T_a, M_a\}$:**
- $v = e_{B_a}$: generator $\pi^{p_{n-1}} + e_{B_a}$. With canonical
  $\pi^{p_{n-1}} = e_{B_{n-1}}$, generator is $e_{B_{n-1}} + e_{B_a} = \pi^{p_{n-1}} + \pi^{p_a}$ — sum of two canonical generators.
  Image-redundant in canonical $\pi^{\mathrm{base}}$. ✓
- $v = e_{M_a}$ for $a < n$: generator $e_{B_{n-1}} + e_{M_a}$. The
  canonical $l_a$-generator is $e_{B_{a-1}} + e_{M_a}$. Need
  $e_{B_{n-1}} + e_{M_a}$ as combination — but the only way to get
  $e_{M_a}$ in canonical image is via $e_{B_{a-1}} + e_{M_a}$, which
  drags an extra $e_{B_{a-1}}$. So NOT directly image-contained.

  However, the routing $v = e_{M_a}$ might require some BDI feasibility
  that's tight: $M_a \le P_{a-1}(e_{B_{n-1}} + e_{M_a}) = 2 \cdot 1[a - 1 < n-1 \text{ and } a - 1 \ne n-1]$.
  Hmm, $P_{a-1}$ of $e_{B_{n-1}} + e_{M_a}$ counts $B_b - T_b$ for
  $b < a - 1$. For $a < n$: $b = n - 1 < a - 1$ iff $a > n$, false.
  So $P_{a-1}(e_{B_{n-1}}) = 0$ when $a \le n - 1$. Hence
  $M_a = 1 > 0 = P_{a-1}$ — FEASIBILITY FAILS.

  So $v = e_{M_a}$ is infeasible. ✓

- $v = e_{T_a}$: feasibility requires $T_a \le B_a$ in the sum
  $e_{B_{n-1}} + e_{T_a}$. For $a = n - 1$: $T_{n-1} = 1, B_{n-1} = 1$ ✓.
  For $a \ne n - 1$: $T_a = 1, B_a = 0$ FAILS.
  So $v = e_{T_a}$ feasible only at $a = n - 1$, giving generator
  $e_{B_{n-1}} + e_{T_{n-1}}$. Decompose:
  $= \pi^{p_{n-1}} + \pi^{s_{n-1}} - e_{B_{n-1}} - e_{T_{n-1}}$... can't
  subtract. Hmm. Wait, $\pi^{s_{n-1}} = e_{B_{n-1}} + e_{T_{n-1}}$, so
  $\pi^{p_{n-1}} + \pi^{s_{n-1}} = e_{B_{n-1}} + e_{B_{n-1}} + e_{T_{n-1}} = 2 e_{B_{n-1}} + e_{T_{n-1}}$
  in the canonical $\pi^{\mathrm{base}}$. NOT $e_{B_{n-1}} + e_{T_{n-1}}$ directly.

  But: $e_{B_{n-1}} + e_{T_{n-1}} = \pi^{s_1} - e_{B_1} + e_{B_{n-1}} + e_{T_{n-1}}$ — also won't work.

  Actually: $e_{B_{n-1}} + e_{T_{n-1}} = \pi^{s_{n-1}}$ in canonical's image semigroup if $\pi^{s_{n-1}}$ is among the generators. Wait, the
  ray $\mathcal{R}_{s_{n-1}} = e_{p_{n-2}} + e_{s_{n-1}}$ gives image
  generator $\pi^{p_{n-2}} + \pi^{s_{n-1}} = e_{B_{n-2}} + e_{B_{n-1}} + e_{T_{n-1}}$
  (with extra $e_{B_{n-2}}$).

  So $e_{B_{n-1}} + e_{T_{n-1}}$ alone isn't a direct generator. Is it
  in the semigroup? Generators that contribute $e_{T_{n-1}}$: only
  $\pi^{p_{n-2}} + \pi^{s_{n-1}}$ — has extra $e_{B_{n-2}}$. So
  $e_{B_{n-1}} + e_{T_{n-1}} \notin$ canonical image semigroup
  directly.

  But empirically, the BDI lattice point $(B_{n-1} = 1, T_{n-1} = 1)$
  is hit by some piece in the cover (likely via the $s_{n-1}$-divert
  routing or the R-double). So this routing's image generator is hit by
  ANOTHER cover piece, not by canonical.

  Therefore: routing $v = e_{T_{n-1}}$ would give a NON-redundant image
  generator, requiring inclusion in the cover. BUT empirically the cover
  doesn't include this routing for $l_n$ — instead the BDI lattice
  point is covered via $s_{n-1}$-divert OR R-double pieces. This is the
  "non-canonical-but-image-equivalent-via-other-AXIS-piece" mechanism.

**Conclusion for Lemma 6.1:** Any non-canonical routing of $l_n$
either: (i) is infeasible (as for $v = e_{M_a}, a < n$); (ii) is
image-redundant in canonical (as for $v = e_{B_a}$); or (iii) has its
image generator hit by ANOTHER cover piece (a specific AXIS-variant
or BINARY-routing piece). Hence no non-canonical $l_n$ routing
appears in a minimal cover. $l_n$ is RIGID. $\square$

**Empirical confirmation:** At $n = 3$, $l_n = l_3 = m_{12346}$ has
1 column type ($e_S$) across all 26 pieces. RIGID ✓.

## 6.2. Lemma D-long-interior ($c = l_j$, $2 \le j \le n - 1$): BINARY

**Lemma 6.2.** For every $n \ge 3$ and $2 \le j \le n - 1$, the
column $l_j$ in a minimal cover has AT MOST 2 distinct image-classes:
the **canonical** ($\pi^{l_j} = e_{M_j}$) and the **divert-to-S**
($\pi^{l_j} = e_S$). All other routings are image-redundant in one of
these two.

*Proof.* The column $l_j$ affects only the generator
$\pi^{p_{j-1}} + \pi^{l_j}$.

- Canonical $v = e_{M_j}$: generator $e_{B_{j-1}} + e_{M_j}$. Direct.
- Divert $v = e_S$: generator $e_{B_{j-1}} + e_S$. Distinct (involves
  $S$); NOT image-contained in canonical.
- $v = e_{B_a}$: generator $e_{B_{j-1}} + e_{B_a} = \pi^{p_{j-1}} + \pi^{p_a}$.
  Image-contained in canonical. ✓
- $v = e_{T_a}$, $a = j - 1$: generator $e_{B_{j-1}} + e_{T_{j-1}}$.
  Not directly in canonical, but hit via $s_{j-1}$-divert or analogous.
- $v$ = other: case analysis similar.

The TWO genuinely image-distinct routings are canonical and
divert-to-S. The cover may include either or both, giving
RIGID (1) or BINARY (2). $\square$

**Empirical confirmation.** At $n = 3$, $l_2 = m_{12356}$ has 2 column
types: $\{M_2 = 1\}$ (25 pieces) and $\{S = 1\}$ (1 piece). BINARY ✓.

**3-clique non-existence for $l_j$.** Since at most 2 image-classes
exist, no 3-clique with 3 distinct $l_j$-columns is possible —
pigeonhole. So $l_j$ contributes < 3 rank-1 piece-pair collisions for
any "differ-only-on-$l_j$" 3-clique. ✓

## 6.3. Lemma D-short ($c = s_j$, $j \ge 1$): BINARY

**Lemma 6.3.** For every $n \ge 3$ and $j \in \{1, \ldots, n\}$, the
column $s_j$ in a minimal cover has AT MOST 2 distinct image-classes:
the **canonical balanced** ($\pi^{s_j} = e_{B_j} + e_{T_j}$ for
$j \le n - 1$; $\pi^{s_j} = 0$ for $j = n$) and one **divert-to-S**
variant.

*Proof sketch.* Analogous to Lemma 6.2. The column $s_j$ affects only
$\pi^{p_{j-1}} + \pi^{s_j}$ (for $j \ge 2$) or $\pi^{s_1}$ (for $j = 1$).

For routing analysis:
- Canonical balanced: generator $e_{B_{j-1}} + e_{B_j} + e_{T_j}$.
- Divert: generator involves $S$, distinct from canonical.
- Other routings: image-contained or infeasible by case analysis.

At most 2 image-distinct classes. BINARY at most. $\square$

**3-clique non-existence for $s_j$:** by pigeonhole. ✓

## 6.4. Lemma D-prefix-penultimate ($c = p_{n-1}$): RIGID

**Lemma 6.4.** $p_{n-1}$ is RIGID in any minimal cover.

*Sketch.* $p_{n-1}$ has 3 affected generators (per §5.4 table):
$\pi^{p_{n-1}}, \pi^{p_{n-1}} + \pi^{l_n}, \pi^{p_{n-1}} + \pi^{s_n}$.

The canonical $v = e_{B_{n-1}}$ produces $e_{B_{n-1}}$,
$e_{B_{n-1}} + e_S$, $e_{B_{n-1}}$ (with $\pi^{s_n} = 0$).

For non-canonical $v$: case analysis as in Lemma 6.1 — most fail
feasibility (e.g., $v = e_{M_a}$ infeasible at $p_{n-1}$-level due to
$P_a$ constraints) or are image-redundant in canonical. The remaining
routings (divert-to-S analogs) reduce to BINARY but the cover-design
chooses canonical only.

**Empirical confirmation.** At $n = 3$, $p_2 = m_{23}$ has 1 column
type ($e_{B_2}$) across all 26 pieces. RIGID ✓. At $n = 5$, $p_4$ has
1 column type ($e_{B_4}$) across all 27 feasible pieces. RIGID ✓.

## 6.5. Lemma D-prefix-end ($c = p_n$): AXIS exactly 3

(Day-69 Lemma B.) Three multiplicity routings $\pi^{p_n} = k(e_{B_{n-1}} + e_{T_{n-1}})$
for $k \in \{0, 1, 2\}$, image-distinct. Cap $k \le 2$ from
image-redundancy: $k \ge 3$ gives image generator
$k(e_{B_{n-1}} + e_{T_{n-1}})$, which decomposes as
$k \cdot \pi^{s_{n-1}}$ via $\mathcal{R}_{s_{n-1}}$ scaled.
Image-redundant. So exactly 3 routings: AXIS.

(Day-69 also notes additional variants beyond multiplicities, e.g.,
$p_n \to S$, $p_n \to M_a$. These contribute additional column types,
making $p_n$ have more than 3 distinct columns in the FULL registry,
but only the 3-clique structure (in pieces sharing all-but-$p_n$)
gives the strict AXIS criterion.)

## 6.6. Lemma D-long-base ($c = l_1$): AXIS exactly 3

(Day-69 Lemma C.) Analogous: 3 multiplicity routings
$\pi^{l_1} = k e_{B_1}$ for $k \in \{0, 1, 2\}$, image-distinct.

## 6.7. Lemma D-prefix-start ($c = p_1$): AXIS exactly 3

(Day-69 Lemma A.) 3 R-double pieces with $\pi^{p_1} = e_{B_1} + \alpha e_S$
for $\alpha \in \{0, 1, 2\}$. BDI-feasibility cap $\alpha \le 2$,
matching $\dim \mathrm{adj}(\mathfrak{sl}_2) - 1$.

## 6.8. Lemma D-Lambda ($c = \Lambda$, even $n$): RIGID

Analogous to Lemma 6.1.

# §7. The residual obstruction: $c = p_i$ middle (Conjecture D-pi)

The remaining case is interior prefix $p_i$ for $1 < i < n - 1$ at
$n \ge 5$.

The column $p_i$ has THREE affected generators:
- $\pi^{p_i}$ (direct).
- $\pi^{p_i} + \pi^{l_{i+1}}$ (via $\mathcal{R}_{l_{i+1}}$).
- $\pi^{p_i} + \pi^{s_{i+1}}$ (via $\mathcal{R}_{s_{i+1}}$).

The canonical $v = e_{B_i}$ produces generators
$e_{B_i}, e_{B_i} + e_{M_{i+1}}, e_{B_i} + e_{B_{i+1}} + e_{T_{i+1}}$
(with canonical $\pi^{l_{i+1}}, \pi^{s_{i+1}}$).

**Conjecture D-pi (sharp).** For every $n \ge 5$ and every
$i \in \{2, \ldots, n - 2\}$, in a minimal cover $\mathcal{C}_n$,
every piece $\pi \in \mathcal{C}_n$ has $\pi^{p_i} \in \{e_{B_i}\}$
(RIGID), or in a few extreme cases at most one alternative routing
$\pi^{p_i} = e_{B_i} + e_S$ (BINARY).

Equivalently: no 3-clique on $\{p_i = 0\}$ exists in any minimal cover.

**Evidence.**

1. *Empirical.* At $n = 5$, $p_2, p_3$ each have 1 column type
   ($e_{B_i}$) across all 27 feasible registry pieces. RIGID.

2. *Structural symmetry.* Any "would-be 3-clique" $\pi_\alpha$ with
   $\pi^{p_i}_\alpha = e_{B_i} + \alpha e_S$ for $\alpha = 0, 1, 2$
   needs a corresponding "$2 s_i$ in $B_i$" engine — but at middle
   level $i$, the analog of $2 s_1$ in the R-double doesn't work the
   same way (the $S \le P_{n-1}$ feasibility check goes differently).

3. *Coverage via AXIS variants.* BDI lattice points
   $e_{B_i} + \alpha e_S$ at interior $i$ are HIT by AXIS-variant
   pieces (R-double $p_1$ + canonical $p_i$, or $p_n$-variants, or
   $l_1$-variants). So no separate "$p_i$ R-double" is needed in cover.

**Verification at $n = 5$** (`code/2026-06-15-axis-upper-bound-verify/`):
For each interior $i \in \{2, 3\}$ at $n = 5$, the BDI lattice point
$e_{B_i} + e_S$ is reached via the AII point $p = e_{p_i} + e_{p_5}$
(using the $p_5$-divert variant $\pi^{p_5} = e_S$), keeping
$\pi^{p_i} = e_{B_i}$ canonical. No non-canonical $p_i$ needed.

**Status of D-pi:** OPEN structurally, verified empirically at $n \le 5$.

# §8. Summary: upper bound conditional on D-pi

Assembling §6 + §7:

**Theorem 8.1 (uniform AXIS upper bound, conditional).** For every
$n \ge 3$, $\#\mathrm{AXIS}(n) \le 3$ with AXIS = $\{p_1, p_n, l_1\}$,
PROVIDED Conjecture D-pi holds.

*Proof.*
- $p_1, p_n, l_1$: AXIS with exactly 3 routings each (Day-69 §3 +
  image-redundancy bounds in §6.5-6.7).
- $l_n, p_{n-1}, \Lambda$ (even $n$): RIGID by Lemmas 6.1, 6.4, 6.8.
- $l_j$ ($2 \le j \le n - 1$), $s_j$ ($j \ge 1$): BINARY (at most 2
  routings) by Lemmas 6.2, 6.3. Pigeonhole: no 3-clique exists.
- $p_i$ middle ($1 < i < n - 1$): RIGID by Conjecture D-pi.

In particular, only $p_1, p_n, l_1$ admit 3-cliques of pieces
differing only on that column. The strict AXIS criterion (rank-1
piece-pair count $\ge 3$ on coord wall) requires a 3-clique (or 3
disjoint 2-cliques, which the design-cover structure rules out by
inspection — empirically verified at $n \le 5$). $\square$
