# Notes on Gutiérrez, "Bender–Knuth involutions for types B and C"

## Bibliographic info
- **Title**: Bender–Knuth involutions for types B and C
- **Author**: Álvaro Gutiérrez (Bonn / Bristol; MSc advisors Stroppel & Matherne; PhD advisor Mark Wildon)
- **arXiv**: 2311.10659 (v3, dated May 28, 2024; arXiv timestamp 20 Sep 2025)
- **Venue**: Electronic Journal of Combinatorics, DOI 10.37236/12571
- **Length**: 12 pages

## Abstract (verbatim)
> We show that the combinatorial definitions of King and Sundaram of the symmetric polynomials of types B and C are indeed symmetric, in the sense that they are invariant by the action of the Weyl groups. Our proof is combinatorial and inspired by Bender and Knuth's classic involutions for type A.

## One-line summary
Gutiérrez constructs explicit involutions on **King (symplectic) and Sundaram (orthogonal) Gelfand–Tsetlin-like patterns** that realize the action of each generator of the Weyl groups $W(B_n) = W(C_n) \cong S_2 \wr S_n$, thereby giving the first correct combinatorial proof that the King/Sundaram generating functions are genuinely Weyl-symmetric (a result claimed but never correctly proved by Sundaram 1986).

## The Bender–Knuth involution: precise statement

### Setup
- $W(B_n) = W(C_n) \subset S_{2n}$, viewed as permutations of $\{1 < \bar 1 < 2 < \bar 2 < \dots < n < \bar n\}$, generated by:
  - $(1\, \bar 1)$ — sign-change on first coordinate,
  - $(j\, j{+}1)(\bar j\, \overline{j{+}1})$ for $j \in [n-1]$ — adjacent transpositions.
- King symplectic pattern: a GT pattern of size $2n$ with $p_{i,j} = 0$ whenever $2i > j$ ("half-triangular").
- Sundaram orthogonal pattern: a King pattern whose top-row entries may be circled (records an extra "$\infty$" entry).
- Weight of a pattern $P$ of type B/C: $x^P = \prod_j x_j^{2S_{2j-1} - S_{2j} - S_{2j-2}}$ where $S_k = \sum_i p_{ik}$.

### The involution $BK^C_j$ (Definition before Prop. 4.3)
For the generator $(1\,\bar 1)$, use $BK^A_1$ (the classical Bender–Knuth on row 1).

For the generator $\sigma_j := (j\, j{+}1)(\bar j\, \overline{j{+}1})$, write
$$\sigma_j = (\bar j\, \overline{j{+}1})(j\, j{+}1)(\bar j\, \overline{j{+}1})(j\, j{+}1)$$
(reading right-to-left as a product of simple transpositions in $S_{2n}$). Apply the corresponding sequence of **type-A** Bender–Knuth involutions $BK^A_{2j}, BK^A_{2j-1}, BK^A_{2j+1}, BK^A_{2j}$ (in that order) to rows $2j, 2j\pm 1$. This produces a pattern of the correct weight but that may **fail to be half-triangular** at one entry $p_{j+1, 2j}$.

Define the **rectification** $\mathrm{rect}$: subtract the offending value $p_{j+1,2j}$ from each of $p_{j+1,2j}, p_{j+1,2j+1}, p_{j,2j}, p_{j,2j-1}$.

Then
$$BK^C_j := \mathrm{rect} \circ BK^A_{2j} \circ BK^A_{2j+1} \circ BK^A_{2j-1} \circ BK^A_{2j}.$$

**Proposition 4.3 (Gutiérrez):** $BK^C_j$ is an involution on King symplectic patterns of fixed top row $\lambda$, and exchanges the weight monomials according to the Weyl-group generator $\sigma_j$.

### Type B
**Corollary 4.7**: $W(B_n) = W(C_n)$, and the orthogonal polynomial is recovered as $o_\lambda = \sum_\mu sp_\mu$. The type-B Bender–Knuth involution is obtained from the type-C one by simply **ignoring the circles** on the top row, applying $BK^C_j$, then putting the circles back. So at the level of involutions, types B and C are essentially the same; the difference is only the circle decoration on the top row of the pattern.

### Translation to tableaux (King symplectic / Sundaram orthogonal tableaux)
On the tableau side, $BK^C_j$ acts by:
1. Performing the four type-A Bender–Knuth involutions on the alphabet positions $\{2j-1, 2j, 2j+1, \overline{2j-1}, \overline{2j}, \overline{2j+1}\}$ (treating the symplectic tableau as a semistandard tableau in $\mathcal A = \{1<\bar1<\dots<n<\bar n\}$).
2. Rectification: relabel any leftover $\{j,\bar j\}$-vertical dominoes between rows $j$ and $j+1$ into $\{j+1, \overline{j+1}\}$-vertical dominoes, and re-sort rows $j, j+1$ to be increasing.

### Invariants preserved
- **Shape** $\lambda$ (top row of the pattern, equivalently shape of the tableau).
- **Half-triangularity / symplectic-or-orthogonal property** of the pattern (after rectification).
- Class of "King symplectic" or "Sundaram orthogonal", respectively.

### Invariants reversed (i.e., acted on by Weyl generator)
- **Weight**: the monomial $x^T$ goes to $\sigma_j \cdot x^T$. Concretely $BK^C_j$ swaps the exponents of $x_j$ and $x_{j+1}$ together with their formal inverses; $BK^A_1$ swaps $x_1$ and $x_1^{-1}$.

### Important non-invariant
Gutiérrez explicitly notes (in introduction, citing his MSc thesis [Gut23, Note 4.11]) that **the involutions do NOT define an action of $S_2 \wr S_n$ on the set of patterns of fixed shape** — only on the polynomial side does the action close up. So $(BK^C_j)^2 = \mathrm{id}$, but the braid relations need not hold pattern-wise.

## The KN-tableau gap (CRUCIAL for Rick)

### Exact quoted passage (p. 2, paragraph 4)
> "On the other hand, crystal bases allow Kashiwara and Nakashima to propose their own tableaux definitions [KN94]. For type A, these recover the above combinatorics; for type C, they are seen to be in bijection with King tableaux in [She99], via an analogue of the jeu de taquin algorithm. **We are not aware of analogous bijections for type B in the literature.**"

So Gutiérrez:
1. Identifies the gap explicitly: **no published bijection between KN tableaux of type $B_n$ and Sundaram orthogonal tableaux of type $B_n$.**
2. By contrast, in type C, Sheats [She99] gave a symplectic jeu de taquin bijection KN-tableaux $\leftrightarrow$ King tableaux. Gutiérrez's $BK^C_j$ on King tableaux therefore *could* be transported through Sheats's bijection to a Bender–Knuth on type-C KN tableaux — but this is not carried out in the paper.
3. In type B, since there is no such bijection, his $BK^B_j$ on Sundaram tableaux does **not** yield a Bender–Knuth on KN type-B tableaux. That is the missing piece.

### Why is it hard?
Gutiérrez does not give a sharp obstruction, but two are implicit:
- The proof that $BK^C_j$ is an involution **already reduces (after a "locality" argument) to an explicit hand computation on a generic pattern with 6 rows / $n=3$ (Section 5)**. The verification is in itself non-trivial (~3 pages of pattern algebra). Any analogous program for KN-tableaux would require a similar combinatorial reduction, but lacking even the bijection KN $\leftrightarrow$ King/Sundaram patterns, one cannot port the proof.
- Gutiérrez stresses (Note 4.11 in [Gut23]) that the involutions $BK^C_j$ **do not satisfy the braid relations on patterns**, so any KN-tableau analogue would inherit the same defect; one cannot demand a clean $W$-action on the set of KN tableaux.
- The addendum shows that, via Grinberg [GR16] and tropicalization, $BK^C_2$ can be checked by computer through detropicalization. This is the only known shortcut, and it does not obviously extend to KN tableaux.

## Bridge to Aug~ and Rick's program

### Comparing invariants
| Object              | Acts on                                  | Preserves        | Reverses                |
|---------------------|------------------------------------------|------------------|-------------------------|
| Gutiérrez $BK^C_j$  | King/Sundaram patterns of shape $\lambda$ | shape $\lambda$  | weight (via $\sigma_j$) |
| Aug~ (Rick)         | (Weyl element $w$, Kostant partition $\pi$) pairs | bidegree $(\ell(w), \deg(\pi))$ | (?)                     |

The most important match is **shape on Gutiérrez's side vs. bidegree on Rick's side**. These are not obviously the same invariant. However:

- Sundaram patterns/tableaux of shape $\lambda$ enumerate a weight-basis of the irreducible $\mathfrak{so}_{2n+1}$-module $V_\lambda$.
- (Weyl element, Kostant partition) pairs at fixed bidegree $(p,q)$ are the basis of the BGG-resolution at chain position $p$ and Verma weight shift $q$.
- If the BGG–Verma differential at bidegree $(p,q)$ is to act as Aug~, it must mix Weyl elements of length $p$ with Kostant partitions of degree $q$. **At fixed bidegree there is no reason a priori for this to align with fixed shape $\lambda$.**

### Is there a natural map KN tableau $\to (w, \pi)$?
This is the key question, and the paper does not address it. Two natural angles:

1. **Weight-space decomposition**: A KN tableau $T \in B(\lambda)$ of weight $\mu$ records a vector in the $\mu$-weight space of $V_\lambda$. If one fixes $\mu$ and lets $\lambda$ vary, one obtains a multiset of dominant weights — but this is precisely a Kostant partition counting (in the spirit of Kostant's multiplicity formula $\dim V_\lambda^\mu = \sum_{w \in W} (-1)^{\ell(w)} K(w(\lambda+\rho) - (\mu+\rho))$). **So $(w, \pi)$ pairs are exactly the indices in Kostant's multiplicity formula**, suggesting a refined map KN-tableau $\to$ (formal alternating sum of $(w, \pi)$ pairs) by the standard BGG/Kostant duality. But this is many-to-many, not a bijection.

2. **Path model / Littelmann paths**: KN type-B tableaux are equivalent to certain Littelmann paths; these in turn relate to W-orbits and Kostant partitions via root operators. This is a more promising bridge but is not in Gutiérrez.

### Assessment: is Aug~ the missing Bender–Knuth?
**Partial.** Reasons in favor:
- Both Aug~ and Gutiérrez's missing $BK^B$ are involutions tied to Weyl-group generators on type-B objects.
- Aug~ preserves bidegree, just as $BK^B$ should preserve shape and reverse weight.
- Aug~ comes from the BGG-Verma differential, which is the algebraic shadow of the combinatorial Weyl symmetry of characters — exactly the thing Gutiérrez is proving by combinatorics. This is a strong conceptual match.

Reasons against / cautions:
- Gutiérrez's involution acts on patterns/tableaux of **fixed shape** $\lambda$. Aug~ acts on $(w, \pi)$ pairs, which by Kostant's formula encode a *signed sum* over all $\lambda$ contributing to a given weight space. They live on different sides of Kostant duality.
- $BK^B$ is one involution per simple reflection $\sigma_j$; Aug~ (as Rick has confirmed for $B_2, B_3$) is the *differential*, a single operator combining all reflections with signs. To get a candidate for $BK^B_j$ from Aug~, one likely needs to restrict/project Aug~ to a single Weyl-direction.
- Gutiérrez's involutions do not satisfy the braid relations, while a BGG differential satisfies $d^2 = 0$. These are compatible only if the projection onto a single reflection direction kills the obstruction.

### Bottom line
**Partial / Promising target.** The KN-tableau Bender–Knuth gap is a precisely-named hole in the literature, and Aug~ has the right type-B/BGG-Verma DNA to fill it — but the matching of invariants (shape vs. bidegree) requires going through Kostant's multiplicity formula and is non-trivial. The most concrete near-term action items:
1. Make explicit the encoding of KN type-B tableaux of shape $\lambda$ in terms of $(w, \pi)$ pairs via Kostant.
2. Check, in $B_2$, whether Aug~ at fixed bidegree $(p,q)$ — restricted in the appropriate way — induces an involution on the KN-tableau side matching Gutiérrez's $BK^B_j$ on the Sundaram side. The smallest interesting case is $\lambda = (1)$ or $(2)$ in $B_2$.
3. If yes, the missing bijection KN $\leftrightarrow$ Sundaram in type B could *itself* drop out of Aug~ (since Aug~ is canonical and BGG-derived).
