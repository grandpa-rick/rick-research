# Lecouvey 2006, Thm 3.2.1 — extraction notes

**Paper.** C. Lecouvey, *Combinatorics of crystal graphs and Kostka–Foulkes polynomials for the root systems B_n, C_n and D_n*, Eur. J. Combin. 27 (2006), 526–557.
**Open access.** arXiv:math/0406574 (PDF: `https://arxiv.org/pdf/math/0406574`). Local copy: `/tmp/lecouvey-2006.pdf`.

## Theorem 3.2.1 (statement)

Let µ ∈ P_{B_n}^+. Then
  Q_µ^{B_n} = Σ_{γ ∈ P_{B_{n-1}}^+} Σ_{R=0}^{∞} Σ_{r+2m=R} q^R Σ_{λ ∈ (γ⊗r)_{B_{n-1}}} b_{γ,r}^λ K_{λ,µ'}^{B_{n-1}}(q) s_{(µ_n + R, γ)},
where µ' = (µ_{n-1},…,µ_1), b_{γ,r}^λ is the multiplicity of B(λ) in B(γ) ⊗ B^{B_n}(r), and (γ⊗r)_{B_{n-1}} = {λ : b_{γ,r}^λ ≠ 0}. Theorem 3.2.2 is the analogous statement for D_n with d_{γ,r}^λ.

This is the orthogonal Morris-recurrence (the type-C version was Thm 3.2.1 of [15]=arXiv:math/0310370). Lecouvey says he only **sketches** other arguments but writes Thms 3.2.1/3.2.2 in detail because they need refinements over the C_n proof.

## Proof structure

Start: Q_µ = (∏_α 1/(1−qR_α)) s_µ (defn) and Nelsen–Ram Prop 3.5 [21] splits the product over R^+_{B_n} into the R^+_{B_{n-1}} part (gives K^{B_{n-1}}_{λ,µ'}(q)) times the "extra" roots in R^+_{B_n} \ R^+_{B_{n-1}}, namely R_i = R_{ε_n−ε_i}, R̄_i = R_{ε_n+ε_i}, R_n = R_{ε_n}.

After applying Thm 2.1.2 and expanding the geometric series in the R_i, R̄_i, R_n directions, one gets
  Q_µ = Σ_{λ ∈ P^+_{B_{n-1}}} K^{B_{n-1}}_{λ,µ'}(q) Σ_{R≥0} q^R Σ_{r=0}^R Σ_{k_•+k̄_•=r (or r−1)} s_{(µ_n + R, γ)},
where γ = (λ_{n-1}+k_{n-1}−k̄_{n-1}, …, λ_1+k_1−k̄_1) and the "−1" comes from absorbing one factor of R_n (the short root). r is the unbarred-letter count, R−r is from R_n^•, with parity r ≡ R mod 2 forced via b in (15)–(16).

**Key step — a sign-reversing involution on multi-indices.** Inside fixed (R,r), write
  S_{R,r} = S_1 + S_2,
S_1 over k_•+k̄_• = r and S_2 over k_•+k̄_• = r−1. Each schur s_{(µ_n+R,γ)} is straightened via the W_{B_n}-dot action. Define on the "non-Pieri" multi-indices a map γ ↦ γ̃ = σ_i ◦ γ:

(a) ∃ i ∈ {1,…,n−2} with λ_i > λ_{i+1} − k_{i+1}: act by simple s_i (swap rows i,i+1 of γ+ρ, decrement i+1, increment i).
(b) λ_• ≤ λ_{•+1} − k_{•+1} for all and λ_1 − k̄_1 < 0 (in S_1): act by s_0 (the short-root reflection).
(c) Same as (b) but in S_2 with λ_1 − k̄_1 ≤ 0: act by s_0.
(d) Pieri conditions hold for s ≠ i but λ_{i+1} − k_{i+1} < λ_i + k_i − k̄_i: act by s_i.

The maps are explicit: e.g. case (a) sets k̃_{i+1} = k_i, k̃_i = k_{i+1}, k̃̄_{i+1} = λ_{i+1} − λ_i + k̄_i + 1, k̃̄_i = λ_i − λ_{i+1} + k̄_{i+1} − 1; case (b) sets k̃̄_1 = k̄_1 − λ_1 − 1, k̃_1 = k_1 + λ_1 (moves S_1 ↔ S_2). Each pairing satisfies s_{(µ_n+R,γ)} = − s_{(µ_n+R,γ̃)}, so all γ in E_a, E_d, E_b, E_c **cancel**.

What survives = exactly the multi-indices satisfying Cor. 3.1.2's conditions (i)–(iii) — i.e. the **Pieri/highest-weight cone**. This survivor sum equals Σ_γ b_{γ,r}^λ s_{(µ_n+R,γ)} by Cor 3.1.2 + Remark (iii) after Cor 3.1.4. The k_0 ∈ {0,1} dichotomy in Cor 3.1.2 produces the parity r ≡ R mod 2 condition (r+2m=R).

## The W → Morris-index bridge

**This is the load-bearing observation for Rick.** The "W-side" alternating sum is over multi-indices (k_1,…,k_{n-1}, k̄_1,…,k̄_{n-1}) — i.e. it's a sum over the orbit of µ+ρ under the relevant translation lattice generated by R_i, R̄_i, R_n. Lecouvey does **not** parametrize the cancelling terms by W elements w; he parametrizes them by **regions** of (k,k̄)-space cut by the inequalities (a)–(d), with each region paired with another via a single simple reflection s_i or s_0.

So:
- The W → Morris-index map is **not given as a global function W → {1,…,n}**.
- It is **piecewise/local**: each non-Pieri multi-index γ is acted on by a **specific s_i** (the *first* i where the Pieri inequality fails, in the cases-(a)/(d)) or by s_0 (cases (b)/(c)).
- The "bridge" is the **survivor characterization** (Cor 3.1.2): only the Pieri-cone multi-indices survive, and these are indexed by L ∈ B^{B_n}(r), i.e. by horizontal-strip data (= Morris index).

**Canonicity verdict.** The involution f (and e) is **canonical given the choice of "first failing i"** convention in (a)/(d). Any reasonable lex/min convention gives the same sign-reversing involution, so the surviving Pieri cone is canonical and the bijection {survivors} ↔ {Pieri/Morris data L} is canonical. **However** the inverse map "Morris index → W element" is not single-valued at the W level: a survivor multi-index does not come with a distinguished w ∈ W_{B_n} attached — it is exactly the term in the original geometric expansion that no s_i ever moved. CKL's pullback to (w,π) therefore picks up an **ambiguity**: at the W-level, multiple lifts (different orderings of the cancellations) could give the same Morris datum. The canonical answer lives one level **down**, on the (k,k̄) multi-index lattice — not on W.

**Bottom line for Route A.** Thm 3.2.1's proof bypasses W entirely after one application of Nelsen–Ram. The W-alternating sum is realized as a (k,k̄)-multi-index sum with a sign-reversing involution by simple reflections, and the survivors are read off as Pieri/Morris data. There is no canonical W → Morris-index function; CKL's pullback to (w,π) is therefore **non-canonical** at the W level (multiple lifts exist), though the resulting Morris-side identity is canonical.
