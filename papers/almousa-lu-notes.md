# Notes on Almousa-Lu, "Ribbon complexes for the 0-Hecke algebra"

**Reference.** Ayah Almousa and Bryan Lu, *Ribbon complexes for the 0-Hecke algebra*, arXiv:2601.13324v1 [math.CO], 19 Jan 2026 (date on paper: January 21, 2026). 24 pages. PDF cached at `/home/agent/projects/papers/almousa-lu-2601.13324.pdf`.

The paper does three things:

1. Constructs split short exact sequences and acyclic cochain complexes of indecomposable projective H_n(0)-modules that lift the ribbon product identity R_α R_β = R_{α·β} + R_{α⊙β} in **NSym**.
2. Proves a Koszulness theorem for an internally graded algebra A built from the tower {H_n(0)}.
3. Defines **skew projective modules** P_{α/β} and identifies their noncommutative Frobenius characteristic with the skewing operator L_β^⊥ R_α on **NSym**, plus a branching SES for β = (1).

The construction in (3) is the one Rick needs.

## 1. Setup and conventions

- Compositions α ⊨ n. Concatenation α·β = (α_1,...,α_ℓ, β_1,...,β_m). Near-concatenation α⊙β = (α_1,...,α_{ℓ-1}, α_ℓ + β_1, β_2,...,β_m).
- For α ⊨ n, **Diag(α)** is the connected ribbon (skew/rim hook) shape with α_i cells in row i (rows numbered from the bottom). A **generalized ribbon** Diag(β⃗) for β⃗ = (β^{(1)}, ..., β^{(ℓ)}) is the disjoint union of connected ribbons Diag(β^{(i)}) stacked strictly to the northeast on the diagonal.
- **Ribbon product identity in NSym** (Eq. (3)): R_α R_β = R_{α·β} + R_{α⊙β}.
- The 0-Hecke algebra H_n(0) is generated by π̄_1,...,π̄_{n-1} (with π̄_i = π_i − 1 so π̄_i^2 = −π̄_i and the braid/commutation relations). For α ⊨ n the indecomposable projective P_α has a tableau basis indexed by **standard ribbon tableaux SRT(α)** of shape Diag(α), with the action defined by Huang [Hua16]:
  - π̄_i(T) = −T if i is in a higher row of T than i+1,
  - π̄_i(T) = 0 if i and i+1 are in the same row,
  - π̄_i(T) = s_i(T) if i is in a lower row than i+1.
- Generalized ribbon projectives P_{β⃗} ≅ (⊗_i P_{β^{(i)}})↑^{H_n(0)}_{H_{n_1,...,n_ℓ}(0)} via parabolic induction.
- Frobenius characteristic ch^**NSym**: K_0(H_•(0)) → **NSym** sends [P_α] ↦ R_α (Krob-Thibon [KT97]). On the simple side ch^**QSym**: G_0(H_•(0)) → **QSym** sends [C_α] ↦ L_α (fundamental basis).

## 2. The split SES (Theorem A / Proposition 5.3)

This is the module-level lift of R_α R_β = R_{α·β} + R_{α⊙β}.

> **Proposition 5.3 (Almousa-Lu).** *For α ∈ Comp(n) and β ∈ Comp(m), one has maps ∂ := ∂_{α,β} and μ := μ_{α,β} giving rise to the following H_{n+m}(0)-equivariant split short exact sequence of H_{n+m}(0)-modules over any field:*
>
> 0 → P_{α·β} →^{∂} P_{(α,β)} →^{μ} P_{α⊙β} → 0.   (Eq. 10)

Here:
- **∂_{α,β}** sends a standard tableau U of shape Diag(α·β) (the connected concatenated ribbon) to the same filling viewed on the disconnected shape Diag(α,β) (β-component translated 1 unit right).
- **μ_{α,β}** sends a standard tableau T of shape Diag(α,β) to T^↓ (β-component translated 1 unit *down*, which produces the near-concatenation Diag(α⊙β)) if T^↓ is a standard ribbon tableau, and to 0 otherwise.

Equivariance (Lemma 5.2) is the only non-formal check; splitting follows because P_{α⊙β} is projective.

## 3. The cochain complex (Construction 5.5 / Theorem 5.6)

**Construction 5.5.** Given α⃗ = (α^{(1)},...,α^{(ℓ)}) with |α⃗| = n, for I ⊆ [ℓ−1] write α⃗(I) for the length-(ℓ−|I|) sequence obtained by replacing the i-th comma of α⃗ with ⊙ for each i ∈ I. Define

  𝒞_i(α⃗) := ⊕_{|I|=i, I ⊆ [ℓ−1]} P_{α⃗(I)},

with differential ∂_i: 𝒞_i → 𝒞_{i+1} given on a tableau T ∈ P_{α⃗(I)} by

  ∂_i(T) := ∑_{j ∈ [ℓ−1]∖I} sgn(j,I) · φ_j(T),

where sgn(j,I) := (−1)^{#{t : i_t < j}} and φ_j applies the gluing of Proposition 5.3 to the adjacent pair of components (j, j+1).

> **Theorem 5.6 (Almousa-Lu).** *For any sequence of compositions α⃗ = (α^{(1)},...,α^{(ℓ)}), the pair (𝒞(α⃗), ∂) is a cochain complex which is acyclic in strictly positive cohomological degrees, and*
>
> H^0(𝒞(α⃗)) ≅ P_{α^{(1)}·α^{(2)}·...·α^{(ℓ)}}.

Proof: induction on ℓ via a mapping-cone SES 0 → 𝒞(α⃗″)[−1] → 𝒞(α⃗) → (𝒞(α⃗′) ⊗ P_{α^{(ℓ)}})↑ → 0.

## 4. Koszulness (Theorem B / Theorem 6.4)

**Construction 6.1.** Let A := ⊕_{n≥0} ⊕_{α ⊨ n} P_α with two gradings: external degree n (the H_n(0) component) and **internal degree** deg_int(P_α) := ℓ(α^⊤) (number of *columns* of Diag(α)). Multiplication m: A ⊗̂ A → A is the induction product whose component on P_α ⊗̂ P_β ≅ P_{(α,β)} is the **near-concatenation map**

  μ_{α,β}: P_{(α,β)} → P_{α⊙β} ⊆ A.

Internal-grading homogeneity: ℓ((α⊙β)^⊤) = ℓ(α^⊤) + ℓ(β^⊤). Generated by A_1 (Lemma 6.2: every Diag(α) is built by ⊙-gluing single-column ribbons (1^{c_j})).

> **Theorem 6.4 (Almousa-Lu).** *The internally graded algebra A from Construction 6.1 is Koszul.*

Proof uses VandeBogert's [Van25] criterion: A is Koszul iff for all α, β the canonical sequence of ribbon Schur modules

  0 → 𝕊_A^{α·β} → 𝕊_A^α ⊗̂ 𝕊_A^β → 𝕊_A^{α⊙β} → 0

is exact. Proposition 6.3 shows that in external degree N this canonical sequence is a direct sum of the split SESs of Proposition 5.3 (indexed by generalized ribbons γ⃗ with prescribed column-length data), and is therefore exact.

## 5. Skew projective modules (Theorem C / Section 7) — the key construction

> **Definition 7.1 (Almousa-Lu).** *Fix α ⊨ n and β ⊨ k ≤ n. Let C_β denote the 1-dimensional simple H_k(0)-module indexed by β, and let*
>
>   C_β^* := Hom_**k**(C_β, **k**)
>
> *be its **k**-linear dual, viewed as a right H_k(0)-module via (φ·a)(v) := φ(a·v). Restrict the H_n(0)-module P_α to the parabolic subalgebra H_{k,n−k}(0) ≅ H_k(0) ⊗_**k** H_{n−k}(0), and denote this restricted module by P_α↓^{H_n(0)}_{H_{k,n−k}(0)}. Define the **skew projective module** to be the left H_{n−k}(0)-module*
>
>   P_{α/β} := C_β^* ⊗_{H_k(0)} (P_α↓^{H_n(0)}_{H_{k,n−k}(0)}).
>
> *Here H_{n−k}(0) acts through the H_{n−k}(0)-factor of H_{k,n−k}(0) on P_α↓, and this action commutes with the right H_k(0)-action used in the tensor product.*

**Composition.** P_{α/β} is built in three steps:
1. *Restrict* P_α from H_n(0) down to the parabolic H_k(0) ⊗ H_{n−k}(0).
2. *Tensor* with the dual simple C_β^* over H_k(0). This **selects/projects out** the β-isotypic component along the H_k(0)-direction (it is the "Hom-out from C_β" up to dualization).
3. The result is left H_{n−k}(0)-module.

**Skew shape:** there is no honest "skew shape Diag(α/β)" appearing as a basis index. The construction is *intrinsic* — Hom-from-C_β applied to the parabolic restriction. Concrete tableau-level decompositions are given only in the row/column cases (Section 7.3): when β = (k), Proposition 7.7 gives P_{α/(k)} ≅ ⊕_{β ∈ weakComp(k,ℓ(α)), (α−β)_i > 0 for i < ℓ(α)} P_{rowDelete(α,β)} (the analogue when β = (1^k) uses column-deletion on α^⊤).

> **Proposition 7.2 (Almousa-Lu).** *Let P_{α/β} be as in Definition 7.1. Then for every finite-dimensional left H_{n−k}(0)-module M there is a natural isomorphism*
>
>   Hom_{H_{n−k}(0)}(P_{α/β}, M) ≅ Hom_{H_n(0)}(P_α, (C_β ⊗ M)↑^{H_n(0)}_{H_{k,n−k}(0)}).
>
> *In particular, P_{α/β} is projective.*

This is the universal property: P_{α/β} corepresents the functor Hom_{H_n(0)}(P_α, (C_β ⊗ −)↑).

> **Proposition 7.3 (Almousa-Lu).** *For α ⊨ n and β ⊨ k with k ≤ n, the noncommutative Frobenius characteristic satisfies*
>
>   ch^**NSym**(P_{α/β}) = L_β^⊥ R_α    in **NSym**.

So skewing identities in **NSym** (which dualize multiplications in **QSym** via the perfect pairing) lift uniformly from K_0 to a level of explicit projective H_{n−k}(0)-modules.

## 6. Branching: the case β = (1)

The case β = (1) is the **restriction along H_n(0) ⊃ H_{1,n−1}(0)**, i.e., "remove one box."

> **Lemma 7.5.** *Fix ↓ := ↓^{H_n(0)}_{H_{1,n−1}(0)}. Then the following diagram commutes:*
>
>   K_0(H_•(0)) →^{ch^**NSym**} **NSym**
>     ↓ (−)↓                       ↓ L_{(1)}^⊥
>   K_0(H_•(0)) →^{ch^**NSym**} **NSym**.

So ch^**NSym** intertwines parabolic restriction (along the smallest non-trivial parabolic) with the L_{(1)}^⊥ skew operator on **NSym**.

> **Proposition 7.12 (Almousa-Lu).** *Let α ⊨ n and let ε_i denote the i-th standard basis vector. For each i ∈ [ℓ(α)] with α_i > 1, the generalized ribbon rowDelete(α, ε_i) has at most two connected components; write its (ordered) list of components as*
>
>   components(rowDelete(α, ε_i)) = (α'_i, α''_i) if disconnected, (α − ε_i) if connected.
>
> *Then there is a split short exact sequence of H_{1,n−1}(0)-modules*
>
>   0 → ⊕_{α_i > 1} P_{(1)} ⊗ P_{α − ε_i} →^{∂} P_α↓^{H_n(0)}_{H_{1,n−1}(0)} →^{μ} ⊕_{α_j^⊤ > 1} P_{(1)} ⊗ P_{(α^⊤ − ε_j)^⊤} → 0.
>
> *On disconnected summands P_{(1)} ⊗ P_{(α'_i, α''_i)} the maps are 1 ⊗ ∂_{α'_i, α''_i} and 1 ⊗ μ_{α'_i, α''_i} as in Definition 5.1; on connected summands P_{(1)} ⊗ P_{α − ε_i} the map ∂ is the natural inclusion.*

**Corollary 7.13.** *L_{(1)}^⊥ R_α = ∑_{α_i > 1} R_{α − ε_i} + ∑_{α_j^⊤ > 1} R_{(α^⊤ − ε_j)^⊤}.*

**Plain English.** Restriction of P_α along H_n(0) ⊃ H_{1,n−1}(0) is a projective H_{1,n−1}(0)-module that fits into a *split short exact sequence* whose terms are direct sums of P_{(1)} ⊗ P_β. This is exactly "Δ on **NSym** lifted to a module-level SES" for the case of restricting one box.

## 7. What this means for Krob-Thibon Phase 5

Rick's Phase 5 (in `/home/agent/projects/proofs/2026-05-06-h0-hecke-nsym.md`, lines 189–272) needed three things, which the gap at line 260 acknowledges:

(a) Res_{m,n−m} P_γ is projective as H_{m,n−m}(0)-module, so that Δ[P_γ] := [Res P_γ] is well-defined in K_0^{proj}(H_m(0)) ⊗ K_0^{proj}(H_{n−m}(0)).

(b) The decomposition of [Res P_γ] in K_0^{proj} has a closed form matching the **NSym** ribbon coproduct:

  Δ R_γ = ∑_{(α,β): γ ∈ {α·β, α⊙β}} R_α ⊗ R_β.

(c) An honest module-level (not just K_0-level) lift, so that the comultiplication is realized by an *actual* short exact sequence of projectives — categorifying Δ_**NSym**.

The Phase-5 gap in Rick's draft was specifically the appeal to "Coind ⊣ Res plus Frobenius extension, taken on faith." Almousa-Lu give an alternative route that bypasses Coind entirely:

- **(a) is proved directly** by the parabolic-freeness lemma (which Rick already has). Almousa-Lu also use this — see the proof of Lemma 7.5 ("By work of Norton [Nor79], H_n(0) is free (hence projective) as a right module over any parabolic subalgebra H_{k,n−k}(0). Consequently, restriction along H_{1,n−1}(0) ⊂ H_n(0) sends projective modules to projective modules.")
- **(b) is Corollary 7.13** for the β = (1) generator of the skewing operator, and Proposition 7.3 (ch^**NSym**(P_{α/β}) = L_β^⊥ R_α) globally.
- **(c) — the categorification — is Proposition 7.12** for the single-box case and the cochain complex of Construction 5.5 for iterated skewings.

### The skew projective module realizing Δ at the module level

**The skew projective P_{α/β} categorifies the (β-strand of the) coproduct of R_α in NSym.** Concretely:

- The β = (1) case gives the **single-box restriction** (one strand of Δ): P_{α/(1)} models the H_{1,n−1}(0)-restriction of P_α, and Proposition 7.12 gives a *split SES* of projective H_{1,n−1}(0)-modules whose terms are P_{(1)} ⊗ P_β over allowable disconnected/connected single-box deletions.
- Iterating: for restriction along H_{k,n−k}(0), the skew projective P_{α/(k)} (and more generally P_{α/β} for any β ⊨ k) gives the *projective H_{n−k}(0)-summand* obtained by Hom-out from C_β. By Frobenius reciprocity (Proposition 7.2):
  
    Hom_{H_{n−k}(0)}(P_{α/β}, M) ≅ Hom_{H_n(0)}(P_α, (C_β ⊗ M)↑).
  
  This is exactly the adjoint identity Rick was trying to invoke as "Coind ⊣ Res", but with C_β^* explicitly replacing the abstract Coind functor — no Frobenius-extension black box required.
- The full coproduct Δ[P_γ] = ∑_m [Res_{m,n−m} P_γ] is recovered as ∑_{α,β} (multiplicity of P_α ⊗ P_β in Res P_γ) · [P_α ⊗ P_β], where the multiplicity equals the coefficient of L_α in L_β L_γ in **QSym** (Remark 7.4) — a shuffle count.

### How to write up Phase 5 closure

The cleanest writeup substitutes the abstract "Coind ⊣ Res / Frobenius extension" appeal with:

1. *Citation of Almousa-Lu Definition 7.1*: introduce the skew projective module P_{α/β} = C_β^* ⊗_{H_k(0)} (P_α↓).
2. *Citation of Almousa-Lu Proposition 7.2*: this gives the desired Frobenius-reciprocity-style adjunction
     dim Hom_{H_{n−k}(0)}(P_{α/β}, C_γ) = dim Hom_{H_n(0)}(P_α, (C_β ⊗ C_γ)↑),
   and shows P_{α/β} is projective. **No Frobenius-extension hypothesis needed.**
3. *Citation of Almousa-Lu Proposition 7.3*: ch^**NSym**(P_{α/β}) = L_β^⊥ R_α gives Δ at the level of characteristics.
4. *Citation of Almousa-Lu Proposition 7.12*: explicit module-level SES for the β = (1) (single-box) case — this is the *atomic* categorification of Δ.

For Rick's purposes the cleanest closure is: define [Res_{k,n−k} P_γ] via the universal property of P_{γ/β} (which is computed by Hom(P_α, (C_β ⊗ C_γ)↑) on the K_0 pairing side), invoke Proposition 7.3 to identify ch^**NSym** with L_β^⊥ R_γ, and use the standard **NSym**/**QSym** duality to identify L_β^⊥ R_γ with the (β,γ)-strand of Δ R_γ on the ribbon basis.

## 8. Surprises / things to know

- **There is no skew shape Diag(α/β) appearing as a basis index** in the general definition. The construction is *intrinsic* (Hom-from-C_β), not combinatorial. Tableau-level decompositions of P_{α/β} are given only when β is a single row (k) or a single column (1^k), via row-deletion / column-deletion (Proposition 7.7). For general β the structure of P_{α/β} as a sum of indecomposables is computed by *iterating* Theorem 5.6 (Examples 7.9 and 7.10).
- **The cochain complex 𝒞(α⃗) of Construction 5.5 categorifies the ribbon-product expansion of R_{α^{(1)}} R_{α^{(2)}} ··· R_{α^{(ℓ)}}** in **NSym** — i.e., the iterated Eq. (3) — not the coproduct. The coproduct categorification is via skew projectives, which is a separate piece of the paper.
- **The Koszul algebra A = ⊕_{n,α ⊨ n} P_α** is *not* the 0-Hecke algebra itself: it is built from the *projective modules* of the tower, with multiplication = near-concatenation gluing (μ_{α,β}). Internal grading is the column-count ℓ(α^⊤). The internal grading is essential — the algebra is noncommutative and infinite-dimensional, and Koszulness is not a finite-dimensional statement.
- **Almousa-Lu's adjunction in Proposition 7.2 is not Frobenius reciprocity in the textbook sense.** It is a tensor-Hom adjunction over H_k(0) combined with parabolic induction; their proof (Eqs. 18–20) goes through finite-dimensionality of C_β to get C_β ⊗ M ≅ Hom_**k**(C_β^*, M) and then standard tensor-Hom. This *is* effectively a Frobenius-extension argument (since H_n(0) is free over H_{k,n−k}(0)) but is presented concretely without invoking the abstract Frobenius-extension formalism.
- **The role of α^⊤ (transpose composition).** Several constructions use ℓ(α^⊤) (column count) rather than ℓ(α) (row count). The reason: near-concatenation glues ribbons *horizontally* (adding columns), so column count is the natural homogeneous degree for the algebra A. For row-skewing P_{α/(k)} the deletion is in *rows* of α, but for column-skewing P_{α/(1^k)} the deletion is in rows of α^⊤ — i.e., columns of α. The duality is built into the definitions.
- **The single-box branching SES (Proposition 7.12) is split.** This is stronger than a general SES — it gives an outright direct-sum decomposition of P_α↓^{H_n(0)}_{H_{1,n−1}(0)} as an H_{1,n−1}(0)-module. The split structure is what makes the K_0-level identity into an honest module-level identity.
- **Ribbon Schur module criterion (VandeBogert 2025).** The Koszulness proof leans on VandeBogert's recent paper [Van25] (arXiv 2025), which characterizes Koszulness of standard internally graded algebras in terms of exactness of canonical ribbon Schur module sequences. This is a recent technology — worth citing if Rick wants to import the Koszul side.
