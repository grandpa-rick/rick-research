STEP 1: Verify palindromy of ALL linear projections of B_3, C_3 weights
======================================================================

  ✓ B_3 adj+triv: all 2000 random integer projections give palindromic histograms.
  ✓ C_3 adj+triv: all 2000 random integer projections give palindromic histograms.

THEOREM (palindromy).  For B_3, C_3, the longest element w_0 of the
Weyl group acts as -1 on the weight space.  Therefore the multiset of
weights of adj ⊕ triv is invariant under negation.  Hence, for ANY
linear projection P : ℝ³ → ℝ, the level-set sizes #{w : P(w) = k}
are palindromic: #{P(w) = k} = #{P(-w) = -k} = #{P(w') = -k} where
w' = -w runs over the same multiset.

STEP 3: Refutation — sharp form
======================================================================

Claim.  There is NO bijection Φ : {22 Bucket-2 pieces} → adj(g)⊕triv
for g = B_3 or C_3, together with three linear functionals f_1, f_2,
f_3 on the weight space of g, such that f_k(Φ(p)) = c_k(p) for
c_k(p) the column-index of piece p along axis k ∈ {m_2, m_236, m_23456}.

Proof.  If such Φ existed, the multiset {(c_1(p), c_2(p), c_3(p))}
would equal {(f_1(w), f_2(w), f_3(w)) : w ∈ adj(g)⊕triv} as multisets.
Projecting onto axis k, the multiset of level-set sizes for the column-
index would equal the level-set sizes of f_k applied to the weight
multiset.  Since adj(g)⊕triv weights are invariant under w ↦ -w (because
-1 ∈ W(B_3) = W(C_3)), and f_k is linear, the f_k-level-set multiset
is invariant under k ↔ -k → it's PALINDROMIC.

But our column-index marginals are NOT palindromic (Step 2).  Contradiction.

∎

Corollary.  The rep-theoretic identification by adjoint-orbit labelling,
for any rank-3 semisimple Lie algebra of dimension 21, is refuted in
the coordinate-respecting sense.

Notes.
 (i)  Other rank-3 candidates: A_3=D_3 has dim 15 (≠ 21), G_2 has rank 2,
      F_4 has rank 4.  So B_3 and C_3 are the only candidates.
 (ii) The refutation extends to any Lie algebra with -1 ∈ W; that's
      types B_n, C_n, D_2k, E_7, E_8, F_4, G_2.  A_n (n>1), D_{2k+1},
      E_6 lack -1, so this argument doesn't apply, but those have
      different dim too.

======================================================================

  i2=0: (M_2, S) = (0, 0)   m_2 → B_1
  i2=1: (M_2, S) = (0, 1)   m_2 → B_1 + S
  i2=2: (M_2, S) = (0, 2)   m_2 → B_1 + 2S

  → a 'staircase' shape in (M_2, S): (0,0) → (0,1) → (0,2) → (1,2).

The 8 m_23456 columns: 6 form a 2×3 grid (M_2 ∈ {0,1}) × (S ∈ {0,1,2}),
plus 2 'B_2,T_2-mass' outliers (i23456=0 with no B_1, i23456=7 with
B_2+T_2 + M_2+B_1).

The 9 m_236 columns split 5 + 4 by M_2 presence.  The 5 M_2-free
columns are (B_1, T_1, B_2, T_2) ∈ {(0,0,1,1), (1,1,0,0), (1,1,1,1),
(1,1,2,2), (2,2,1,1)}: a 5-element set in (a,a,b,b) form where (a,b)
∈ {(0,1),(1,0),(1,1),(1,2),(2,1)} — the lattice points of a 'diamond'.

These three column structures have specific BDI/AII coordinate
origins (M_2 + S substitutions, R-double structures, transpose-related
'21/12' families) traceable to the Day-58 minimal-cover construction.

The configuration is therefore a 'BDI/AII coordinate-substitution graph'
rather than a Lie-theoretic object.  This is what OQ-PI3-MULTI-FINAL
captures: the structural invariant is intrinsic combinatorics, not rep
theory.
