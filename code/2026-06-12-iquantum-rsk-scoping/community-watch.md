# Question

Is the synthesis "spectral realisation of RSK$^{A\mathrm{II}}$ via
degenerate / affine ι-Hecke" already in print, in preparation, or has
anyone announced it?

# Quick verdict

**No, as of June 2026 the synthesis is not in print.**

But several research groups are close and could publish first:

# Who to watch

## (W1) Hideya Watanabe (RIMS Kyoto)

- Wrote arXiv:2509.00853 (Berele row-insertion via QSP, Sept 2025).
- Prior work: arXiv:2307.01437 (Watanabe '23, "Symplectic tableaux and
  quantum symmetric pairs") — the foundational paper on
  $\{b^\iota_T\}$ canonical-like basis.
- arXiv:2105.04691 (Watanabe '21, "Classical weight modules over
  ıquantum groups") — sets up the classical-weight framework.
- **Likelihood of writing the synthesis: very high.** He has the QSP
  machinery, knows OT combinatorics, and the paper explicitly leaves
  spectral interpretation open.
- **Recommendation:** if pursuing the synthesis, coordinate with
  Watanabe early to avoid duplicate work.

## (W2) Eugene Stern

- Wrote arXiv:2606.00679 (AHA! RSK, May 2026).
- His paper has no type-B/C content, but the abstract hints at
  generalising the spectral approach to other Hecke types.
- **Likelihood of writing the synthesis: medium.** Stern is an
  amateur (the paper is dedicated to Kirillov on his 90th birthday,
  signalling a personal project). He may or may not pursue type-AII.
- Hard to predict — keep an eye on his arXiv listings, but lower
  priority than (W1).

## (W3) Huanchen Bao, Weiqiang Wang, and collaborators

- The "iquantum" / "ı-Schur duality" research programme.
- Key papers:
  - arXiv:1610.09271 (Bao–Wang, "Quantum symmetric pairs of any
    finite type and Schur duality").
  - arXiv:1907.13362 (Bao–Sun, "Schur duality for the
    affine ı-Hecke algebra") — **this is the affine setting**!
  - arXiv:2305.05625 (Bao–Wang, recent ı-RSK / canonical basis updates).
- **Likelihood: medium-high.** Bao–Wang's affine ı-Hecke is exactly
  the (B1) input for the synthesis. If they're interested in RSK they
  could write it; but their work has trended toward canonical bases
  and category-O analogues recently.
- **Recommendation:** carefully read arXiv:1907.13362 before starting —
  it may already contain the iquantum-JM elements.

## (W4) Toya Hiroshima, Hiraku Nakajima group (RIMS)

- arXiv:1912.00204 (Hiroshima, on King tableaux and RSK).
- Cited by Watanabe '25; the RSK-on-King-tableaux paper is structurally
  adjacent.
- **Likelihood: low for synthesis.** Hiroshima is on the combinatorial
  side; less likely to produce a spectral interpretation.

## (W5) Pavel Etingof / MIT

- Long-standing interest in $\imath$quantum groups and Hecke duality.
- arXiv:2303.10412 (Etingof–Mbele) on stuff adjacent to this.
- **Likelihood: low–medium.** Etingof is a wildcard.
